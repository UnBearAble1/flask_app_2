Alchemy Things: 

Quick Alchemy (1 Action): You make a check against the DC of the Alchemical Item to see if you can craft an item. This takes 1 action. 
  • Critical Success:  You create two of the item. They must be activated by the end of your next turn, or they become inert. 
  • Success: You create one of the item. It must be activated by the end of your next turn, or it becomes inert. 
  • Failure: You fail to create the item, and are at a cumulative -2 to try again until you get a result other than Failure. You can reset this penalty by spending ¼ the Complexity value in hours re-sorting your components and recalculating your formulas. The penalty also goes away during your next daily preparations. 
  • Critical Failure: You cannot create this item until you spend a number of hours equal to twice the Complexity value gathering components and recalculating your formulas. This time can be distributed out over multiple days if needed.  

Advanced Alchemy: During your Daily Preparation, Alchemists get 10 Advanced Alchemy points.  These points have two uses: 
  • Re-rolling Quick Alchemy Checks: As a free action, you can spend from your Advanced Alchemy points to re-roll a success or a failure on the Quick Alchemy roll, or to turn a Critical Failure into a regular failure (you can’t do both in a single round). The point cost to do so is equal to the Complexity rating of the formula. 
  • Create Persistent Items: During your daily preparations, you can use your Advanced Alchemy points to make some quick alchemical items that remain potent until your NEXT daily preparations. To do so, simply declare that is what you are doing, and make a roll as normal. Any of these items you have successfully crafted reduces your total AA points by the complexity value. You can only attempt to do so once during your daily preparations, unless you spend a single AA point to allow you to try again.
