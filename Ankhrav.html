Threat: 4 

These horse-sized, burrowing monsters generally avoid heavily settled areas like cities, but ankhravs’ predilection for livestock and humanoid flesh ensures that the creatures do not remain in the deep wilderness for long. Desperate farmers whose fields become infested by ankhravs often have little recourse but to seek the aid of adventurers.

Recall Knowledge - Animal (Nature)

N, Large, Animal, Striker 
Perception +8; darkvision, tremorsense (imprecise) 60 feet
Skills Acrobatics +5, Athletics +8, Stealth +5
AC 19; Fort +8, Ref +9, Will +7
HP 60
Speed 25 feet, burrow 20 feet

Actions: 
  • Mandibles; Melee Single Action;  +9 [+4/+-1] (acid), Damage 2d8+6 piercing plus 2d4 acid
  • Acid Spit; Ranged Single Action; +9 [+4/-1] (acid, range 30 feet), Damage 2d8+6 acid, 1d4 persistent acid damage 
  • Armor-Rending Bite; Two Actions The ankhrav makes a mandibles Strike; if the Strike hits, the target’s armor takes the damage and the acid damage bypasses the armor’s Hardness and DR.
  • Spray Acid Two Actions (acid): Effect The ankhrav spews acid in a 30-foot cone, dealing 3d6 acid damage and 1d6 persistent acid damage (DC 19 basic Reflex save). Recharge: 1d6 rounds
