The world is filled with established weapons and armor with preset traits, but you get to design your own weapons and armor at character creation. Further, depending upon your base, you unlock additional benefits when using weapons or armor, as outline below. 

Armor

Armor provides damage resistance against two of the three following damage types: slashing, bludgeoning, or piercing (at character creation, or if your character has a custom suit of armor made, you get to select which of the two.)  It also may give you speed penalties or added difficulty when using the Stealth or Coordination skills.

  • Light Armor:  Provides DR 1d4. 
  • Medium Armor: Provides DR 1d6.  Gives you a speed penalty of -5 feet.   Has the Inflexible 1 trait (You cannot critically succeed on Stealth or Coordination checks. 
  • Heavy Armor:  Provides DR 1d8.  Gives you a speed penalty of -10 feet. Has the Inflexible 2 trait (As Inflexible 1, but you also treat failures on Stealth and Coordination as Critical failures).  


Armor Traits. Each suit of armor has three armor traits. If your base is adept, you can access the first trait, if hybrid, the first and second, and if warrior all three traits. These are the available armor traits a suit of armor can have: 
  • Mobile: Reduce the speed penalty for this armor by 5 (can be chosen twice) 
  • Flexible: Reduce your Inflexible trait by 1 category (can be chosen twice). 
  • Resilient: You roll two DR dice against critical hits. 
  • Fortified: You get your DR against the third damage type as well. 
  • Concealable: Your armor can be fully concealed within clothing (light armor only) 
  • Comfort: The armor counts as one degree less heavy for the ability to rest in it. This is only beneficial if the armor counts as less than light, then you can rest in it normally. (can be chosen three times). 
  • Spiked: You can make unarmed attacks with this armor that deal 1d6 piercing damage. Additionally, any creature that attempts to grapple you takes 1d6 piercing damage, or 2d6 if they critically fail the attempt. 
  • Insulated: Your DR applies to cold, fire, electricity or acid attacks (pick each time you select this trait) 
  • Impact Resistant: Your DR applies double to falling damage. 

Weapons

Like armor, at character creation you get to design your own weapons. Your character can also have custom weapons crafted that follow the rules here. Otherwise, all other weapons are made with these rules as well, but have static traits based on the game setting. 

The more base damage a weapon does, the fewer traits it gets. The number of traits are also determined by whether the weapon requires one hand or two, or if the weapon is ranged. 

The maximum damage for a 1 handed weapon is 1d8. Ranged weapons that deal 1d10 damage require the reload 1 trait, and those that can get to 1d12 require the reload 2 trait.

Ranged weapons have a default range of 40 feet, and Reload 0 (meaning they can be reloaded and attack in the same action, but it still counts as an 'interact' action).

Weapon Traits 

Most weapon traits will differ depending upon whether the base of the wielder is an Adept, a Hybrid, or a Warrior. The more martial the character, the more skill they have in unlocking the truest potential from weapon traits.

Not all traits are equal, however.  Some cost more trait "slots" than others. 

Agile (2 slots)

(1 handed weapons only)

This weapon has a reduced multiple attack penalty. 
  • Adept:  MAP -4/-8 
  • Hybrid: MAP -4/-6 
  • Warrior: MAP -3/-6

Attached to Shield (3 slots)

This weapon is an attachment to shields, and can be attacked with as long as you are holding the shield. 

Backstabber (1 slot)

This weapon deals more damage to flat footed targets. 
  • Adept: +1 precision damage to flat footed targets 
  • Hybrid: +2 precision damage to flat footed targets 
  • Warrior: +3 precision damage to flat footed targets

Backswing (1 slot)

You can use the momentum from a missed attack with this weapon to lead into your next attack. After missing with this weapon on your turn, you gain a circumstance bonus to your next attack with this weapon before the end of your turn.
  • Adept: +1 to hit after a miss 
  • Hybrid: +2 to hit after a miss 
  • Warrior: +3 to hit after a miss

Chained (1 slot)

This weapon is of extra use against shields. 
  • Adept: Shield DR is counted as half for a shield block 
  • Hybrid: Raised Shields are -1 AC, Shield DR is counted as half for shield block 
  • Warrior: Raised Shields are -2 AC, Shield DR is counted as half for shield block

Climbing (1 slot)

You can climb with the same hand you hold this weapon in. You may also get Item bonuses to the checks. 
  • Adept: +0
  • Hybrid: +1
  • Warrior: +2

Concealable (1 slot)

This weapon is designed to be inconspicuous or easily concealed. You gain a circumstance bonus to Stealth checks to hide or conceal a weapon with this trait.
  • Adept: +2 to checks to conceal 
  • Hybrid: +3 to checks to conceal 
  • Warrior: +4 to checks to conceal

Deadly (1 slot)

On a critical hit, the weapon adds a weapon damage die of the listed size. Roll this after doubling the weapon's damage.
  • Adept: 1d6 bonus damage on a crit 
  • Hybrid: 1d8 bonus damage on a crit 
  • Warrior: 1d10 bonus damage on a crit

Disarm (1 slot)

This weapon can be used to disarm with, and gains a circumstance bonus to do so. 
  • Adept: +1 to checks to disarm 
  • Hybrid: +2 to checks to disarm 
  • Warrior: +3 to checks to disarm

Feint (1 slot)

Using this weapon gives you a circumstance bonus to the feint maneuver. 
  • Adept: +1 to checks to feint 
  • Hybrid: +2 to checks to feint 
  • Warrior: +3 to checks to feint

Finesse (1 slot)

A target's armor DR or Innate Damage Resistance  is considered lower when subject to a critical hit from this weapon. 
  • Adept: 2 points lower 
  • Hybrid: 5 points lower 
  • Warrior: 8 points lower 

Forceful (1 slot)

You gain damage bonuses for consecutive attacks against the same target within the same round. 
  • Adept: +1 damage (cumulative) on subsequent attacks against the same target
  • Hybrid: +2 damage (cumulative) on subsequent attacks against the same target
  • Warrior: +3 damage (cumulative) on subsequent attacks against the same target

Free Hand (3 slots)

(1 handed weapons only) 

This hand this weapon is in counts as a free hand. 

Grapple (1 slot)

This weapon can be used to grapple with, and gains a circumstance bonus to do so.
  • Adept: +1 to checks to grapple 
  • Hybrid: +2 to checks to grapple 
  • Warrior: +3 to checks to grapple

Hampering (1 slot)

A weapon with the hampering trait includes a disruptive limb or flange. You can use an Interact action to thrash the weapon in a square within the weapons reach. That square becomes difficult terrain until you attack with the weapon, move, would otherwise stop thrashing it, or at the beginning of your next turn.
  • Adept: 1 adjacent square 
  • Hybrid: 2 adjacent squares 
  • Warrior: 3 adjacent squares

Jousting (1 slot)

The weapon is suited for mounted combat with a harness or similar means. When mounted, if you moved at least 10 feet on the action before your attack, add a circumstance bonus to damage for that attack. 
  • Adept: +2 to damage 
  • Hybrid: +3 to damage  
  • Warrior: +4 to damage

Modular (1 slot)

You can pick two sets of traits (each losing 1 to this to trait), and switch between the two for an action. 

Nonlethal (1 slot)

(Bludgeoning Weapons Only) 
  • Adept: This weapon can deal nonlethal damage with no penalty 
  • Hybrid: This weapon can deal nonlethal damage with no penalty, and deal +1 damage when doing so 
  • Warrior: This weapon can deal nonlethal damage with no penalty, and deal +2 damage when doing so

Parry (1 slot)

You can spend a single action to position this weapon defensively, gaining a bonus. 
  • Adept: a +1 circumstance bonus to Guard against a single target until the start of your next turn.
  • Hybrid: a +1 circumstance bonus to Guard and Reflex defenses against single target effects until the start of your next turn. 
  • Warrior: a +1 circumstance bonus to Guard and any Reflex defenses against all targets until the start of your next turn.

Long Range (1 slot)

(Ranged weapons only) 
  • Adept: Your base range is 60 feet 
  • Hybrid: Your base range is 80 feet 
  • Warrior: Your base range is 100 feet

Ranged Trip (2 slots)

(Ranged Weapons Only) 
  • Adept: You can use this weapon to trip at 10 feet away 
  • Hybrid: You can use this weapon to trip at 25 feet away 
  • Warrior: You can use this weapon to trip at 40 feet away
Note: The Long Range trait does not increase this range. 

Reach (2 slots)

This weapon has extra reach. 
  • Adept: This melee weapon makes strikes only to targets at 10 feet away. 
  • Hybrid: Pick one of the following when you select this trait: 
    • This melee weapon makes strikes and maneuvers only to targets at 10 feet away
    • This melee weapon may be used to make strikes at up to 10 feet away
  • Warrior: This melee weapon may be used to make strikes and maneuvers to targets at 10 feet away.

Shove (1 slot)

This weapon can be used to shove with, and gains a circumstance bonus to do so.
  • Adept: +2 to checks to shove 
  • Hybrid: +3 to checks to shove 
  • Warrior: +4 to checks to shove

Sweep (1 slot)

This weapon makes wide sweeping or spinning attacks, making it easier to attack multiple enemies. When you attack with this weapon, you gain a circumstance bonus to your attack roll if you already attempted to attack a different target this turn using this weapon.
  • Adept: +1 to strike a second target in a turn
  • Hybrid: +2 to strike a second target in a turn
  • Warrior: +3 to strike a second target in a turn

Tethered (1 slot)

This weapon is attached to a length of rope or chain that allows you to retrieve it after it has left your hand. If you have a free hand while wielding this weapon, you can use an Interact action to pull the weapon back into your grasp after you have thrown it as a ranged attack or after it has been disarmed (unless it is being held by another creature).
  • Adept: This thrown weapon can be pulled back to you with an interact action 
  • Hybrid: This thrown weapon can be pulled back to you with an interact action or reaction
  • Warrior: This thrown weapon can be pulled back to you with an interact free action 

Thrown (1 slot)

(Can be selected twice, the second time doubling the range). 

This melee weapon can optionally be thrown, giving it a range. 
  • Adept: This weapon can be thrown within 10 feet 
  • Hybrid: This weapon can be thrown within 20 feet 
  • Warrior: This weapon can be thrown within 40 feet

Trip (1 slot)

This weapon can be used to Trip with, and gains a circumstance bonus to do so.  If you critically fail a check to Trip using the weapon, you can drop the weapon to take the effects of a failure instead of a critical failure.
  • Adept: +1 to checks to trip 
  • Hybrid: +2 to checks to trip 
  • Warrior: +3 to checks to trip

Twin (1 slot)


These weapons are used as a pair, complementing each other. When you attack with a twin weapon, you add a circumstance bonus to the damage roll if you have previously attacked with a different weapon of the same type this turn. 
  • Adept: +1 damage (cumulative) 
  • Hybrid: +2 damage (cumulative)
  • Warrior: +3 damage (cumulative)

Two-Hand (1 slot)

(only applicable to 1 handed weapons)

When you grasp this weapon in two hands, you increased its damage value 1 dice category. 

Versatile (1 slot)

 A versatile weapon can be used to deal a different type of damage than that listed in the Damage entry. This trait indicates the alternate damage type.
  • Adept: In order to switch to the alternate damage type, you must shift your grip with an interact action. The switch remains until the end of combat. 
  • Hybrid: Each time you attack you can pick to deal the alternate damage type. 
  • Warrior: You can pick between any of the three weapon damage types (slashing, bludgeoning, or piercing) each time you attack. 

Weapon Complications: 

You can select one or more of the following weapon complications for your weapon to have, doing so providing you with extra slots to select. 

  • Off-Center Balance (Melee Weapons). If you miss with an attack with this weapon, you become clumsy 1 until the beginning of your next turn. (Grants you 2 weapon slots). 
  • Volley (Ranged Weapons). You are -2 to hit targets within 30 feet of you. (Grants you 1 weapon slot). 
  • Reload 1 (Ranged Weapons that don't already have Reload 1 or 2). It takes 1 interact action to reload this weapon and ready it to fire again. (Grants you 2 weapon slots). 
  • Reload 2 (Ranged Weapons that don't already have Reload 1 or 2). It takes 2 interact action to reload this weapon and ready it to fire again. (Grants you 3 weapon slots).
