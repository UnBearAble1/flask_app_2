These effects relate to characters ability to perceive the world, and to make perception checks. 

Automatic Detection


When you come within 10 feet (range increment boost costs are tripled, maximum 60 feet) of something that otherwise requires you to specifically seek for it, you automatically get a secret check to automatically detect its presence. 
  • Illusions. Your check is to disbelieve.  (Base: 4, Mod +5) 
  • A creature that is transformed into another form or is Impersonating a specific creature. (Base: 4, Mod +3) 
  • An enchanted or possessed creature (Base: 4) 
  • Unusual stonework or metallurgy (Mod: 1 ) 
  • Hidden creatures attempting to Avoid Notice nearby. You still need to meet any other requirements to notice a particular creature. (Base: 8, Mod +2) 
  • Haunts and Spirits (Base: 4) 
  • Traps. (Base: 8, Mod +2)
  • Analytical Taste: When eating food or drinking a beverage, you automatically attempt to identify the ingredients, which might alert you to the presence of alterations or additives, such as poisons. The GM rolls a secret Perception check; on a success, you learn that the food or drink was poisoned, but not the specific poison used. (Mod: 1)

Change Seek Area


You can use the Seek action in a different sized area 
  • You can use the Seek action to sense undetected creatures within a 30-foot burst instead of a 15-foot burst. (Base: 4) 
  • As long as you can hear normally, you can use the Seek action to sense undetected creatures in a 60-foot cone instead of a 30-foot cone. (Base: 3)
  • Each time you use the Seek action to find hidden or obscured objects,  you can search for objects in your choice of a 10-foot emanation around you or an adjacent 15-foot-by-15-foot area, rather than a single adjacent 10-foot-by-10-foot area. (Base: 3) 

Enhance Imprecise Senses


[Base: 12]
Activation: Duration 

Your imprecise senses become precise senses.

Imprecise Sense


Default Range: 10 feet

You have a special imprecise sense. Hearing is an imprecise sense—it cannot detect the full range of detail that a precise sense can. You can usually sense a creature automatically with an imprecise sense, but it has the hidden condition instead of the observed condition. It might be undetected by you if it’s using Stealth or is in an environment that distorts the sense, such as a noisy room in the case of hearing. In those cases, you have to use the Seek basic action to detect the creature. At best, an imprecise sense can be used to make an undetected creature (or one you didn’t even know was there) merely hidden—it can’t make the creature observed.
  • Tremorsense: You can feel the vibrations through a solid surface caused by movement. Tremorsense functions only if the detecting creature is on the same surface as the subject, and only if the subject is moving along (or burrowing through) the surface. A creature that moves more than 5 feet per action, or one that casts a spell or makes an attack while on the same ground, can be picked up by the sense. (Mod: 10) 
  • Scent: You can use your sense of smell to determine the exact location of a creature. The GM will usually double the range if you're downwind from the creature or halve the range if you're upwind. (Mod: 8) 
  • Spiritsense: You can sense the spirits of creatures, including living creatures, most non-mindless undead, and haunts within the listed range. It can't detect soulless bodies, constructs, or objects, and like most senses, it doesn't penetrate through solid objects. (Base: 8, Mod +8) 
  • Magic Sense: You can detect magic in the area, and the tradition and sphere of magic for the highest-tier effect within range that you detect.  If multiple effects are equally strong, the GM determines which you learn. As an imprecise sense, you can narrow the magic down to a five foot area.  (Base: 4, Mod +3) 

Penetrating Vision


[Base: 16, Mod +8]
You gain the ability to see through matter. You don't take circumstance penalties to Perception checks caused by the matter, but your attacks and spell attacks don’t phase through the matter. If it is porous enough to attack through (or thin or weak enough) your targeted attacks don't require a flat check to succeed against a target concealed only by such matter or have other penalties.
Suggested: use limits to specify the kind of material, such as the ability to see through vines and foliage

Project Senses


Activation: Duration 
Default Range: Touch 
You can project your senses into another creature. The range of this only applies to activating this effect and engaging with the other creature. Once active, the projection has no range limit. 
You can switch between your own senses, and the projected senses, with a single action that has the concentrate trait. 
Senses: 
  • Sight (Base: 12, Mod +8) 
  • Hearing (Base: 8, Mod +8) 
  • All Senses (Base: 12, Mod +20)
Creature: 
  • Your minion (default) 
  • Any animal (Mod +4) 
  • An inanimate corpse (Base -4) 
  • Any creature (Mod +8) 

Remote Sensing

Activation: Duration 
Linked Skill: Stealth
You can project your senses to a different location, away from your body.  When observing in this fashion, you perceive using all of your ordinary senses - including enhanced senses. You are also capable of being subjected to abilities that have the visual, auditory or olfactory traits that you perceive remotely. When remote sensing, you cannot perceive from your own normal senses. 

Sense Type: 
  • Vision (Base: 16, Mod +8) 
  • Hearing (Base: 12, Mod +8) 
  • Touch (Base; 8) 
  • Smell (Base: 4, Mod +4) 
  • Taste (Base: 4) 
  • All Senses (Base: 16, Mod +34) 
Range of Ability: 
  • 15 feet (Mod -8) 
  • 30 feet (Mod -4) 
  • 60 feet (Default) 
  • 120 feet (Mod +4) 
  • 500 feet (Mod +8) 
  • Planetary, though, must be a location you have visited before. (or if you have the ‘scrying’ modification, use those rules.) (Mod +20) 
Detectability: 
  • When using this ability, there is a visible Indicator easily recognizable as yours (typically this is a ‘phantom’ version of yourself at the location you are remotely sensing). (Mod -6) 
  • When using this ability, there is a visible indicator that is difficult to recognize as yours (a glowing eyeball or some such), but with enough investigation or the right ability could be led back to you. (Default) 
  • Invisible Sensor (can be detected with an ability to see invisibility, but can’t expressly be tied back to you without extremely specific abilities to do so) (Mod +6) 
  • Undetectable, though someone may be able to tell you are using a snooping ability if your body is visible to them (You may need to make a deception check). (Mod +12) 
Modifier: 
  • You can move the sensor up to your speed (Mod +10) 
    • The sensor follows the target as it moves. (Mod +20 instead of +10)
  • Two Way Pane: Your remote viewing is actually done via a portal (typically the size of a window, 3x3 feet or so) that allows anyone perceiving through it to sense from either side. (Mod +10) 
  • Scrying: Instead of picking an area to sense remotely, you put a sensor directly above a specific creature (They get a Will Save)- where it remains static as it was formed.  You can choose a target either by name or by touching one of its possessions or a piece of its body. If you haven't met the target in person, scrying's DC is 2 higher, and if you are unaware of the target's identity (perhaps because you found an unknown creature's fang at a crime scene), the DC is instead 10 higher. (Mod +10) 
  • You can perceive both remotely and using your own normal senses simultaneously (Mod +10)

Share Senses


Activation: Duration 
Default Range: Touch

You can share your senses with another creature. The range of this only applies to activating this effect and engaging with the other creature. Once active, the projection has no range limit. 
When you activate this ability, the other creature can perceive anything you perceive in addition to their own senses. If a creature is undetected to a recipient but observed by the sharing creature, the creature becomes observed by the recipient as well. Typically, the creature is seen as a glowing outline superimposed on its position. This might allow the recipient to target a creature it couldn't otherwise; however, cover and line of effect still might prevent or impede targeting and attacks. This can only improve the recipient's vision, not reduce it; for example, if an enemy was undetected by the sharing creature and observed by a recipient, the recipient would still clearly observe the enemy.
Senses: 
  • Sight (Base: 12, Mod +4) 
  • Hearing (Base: 8, Mod +4) 
  • All Senses (Base: 12, Mod +18)

Tracking


Track Through Air and Water 
[Base: 8] 
Linked Skill: Survival 
You can Track through both air and water, and notice aerial or aquatic “tracks” of all creatures who passed nearby in the last hour 

Prolonged Tracking 
[Base: 4]
Linked Skill: Survival
You don’t need to attempt a new Survival check every hour while Tracking.

Vague Sense


Default Range: 30 feet.

You have a special vague sense. A vague sense is similar to a human’s sense of smell, usually providing some sensation like a tingling on your skin or a heightened sense of anxiety. When in proximity to the subject of your senses, typically 30 feet, you eventually sense their presence, though you might not do so instantly, and you can’t pinpoint their location. You must make perception checks against any creature using a disguise or otherwise trying to hide its presence. If the creature succeeds at its Deception, it is then temporarily immune to your ability for 1 day.
  • You can sense evil (Base: 4, Mod +2) 
  • You can sense good (Base: 3) 
  • You can sense law or sense chaos (Mod: 3) 
  • You can sense the presence of undead (Base: 4, Mod +2) 
  • You can sense creatures in the ethereal planes (Base: 4, Mod +2) 
  • You can sense spirits, including those inside of other objects (Base: 4) 
  • You can sense the presence of magic (Base: 3)

Interacting with Hidden/Concealment Effects


Easier Chance to target Concealed Creatures 
  • You reduce the flat check to target concealed creatures by 2 (Base: 4) 
  • You don't need to succeed at a flat check to target creatures benefiting from concealment. (Base: 12) 

Easier Chance to target Hidden Creatures 
  • You reduce the flat check to target hidden creatures by 2 (Base: 4) 
  • You reduce the flat check to target hidden creatures by 5 (Base: 8) 


Being Targeted by Hidden Creatures 
[Base: 8] 
You’re not flat-footed to creatures that are hidden from you (unless you’re flat-footed to them for reasons other than the hidden condition)


Detect Undetected Creatures 
[Base: 4]
While you are adjacent to an undetected creature whose difficulty to detect is a moderate or lower, it is instead only hidden from you. 


Special Restrictions For All Of the Above: 
  • Useful for Detecting Allies Only (Base: -4)


Detect Allies 
[Mod: 2]
Willing allies that you are aware of within 60 feet that would otherwise be undetected by you are instead hidden from you.

Lowlight and Dark Vision Effects


Low-Light Vision
[Base: 4] (Low tech- Base: 8, Mod +10)
You can see in dim light as though it were bright light, so you ignore the concealed condition due to dim light. 


Darkvision
[Base: 8] (Low tech- Base: 8, Mod +30) 
You can see perfectly well in areas of darkness and dim light, though such vision is in black and white only. Most forms of magical darkness block normal darkvision. 


Greater Darkvision 
[Base: 12] (Low tech- Base: 12, Mod +30)
As Darkvision, but you can see through magical darkness as well.
