Exploration, Concentration, Secret
You attempt to persuade an NPC to support your cause, to believe a truth you are telling, or to consider an alternative to their way of thinking. Choose an NPC, and attempt a skill check to persuade that NPC (Deception if you use guile and manipulation, Diplomacy if you use charm or flattery, or Intimidation if your use force of will or aggression.) The DC, and whether success is possible, depend on the NPC's motivations, their disposition toward you, and the approach you take. 

Critical Success You persuade the NPC to the maximum degree possible with your approach, and their disposition toward you improves by one.
Success You persuade the NPC within the confines of your approach.
Failure You fail to persuade the NPC.
Critical Failure You fail to persuade the NPC, and their disposition worsens toward you by one. 
Persuading an NPC can be done with any skill the DM decides is possible, but each NPC should have best and worst approaches - these can be learned with the Discover Motivations activity. Typically these approaches not only modify the DC, but the possible best and worst possible outcomes as well. 

Typically, the DC for persuade roll is based on how set in their opinion they are on the subject: 
  • No real opinion:  intermediate  
  • Some opinion: Moderate 
  • A definite opinion: Difficult 
  • A strong opinion: Very Difficult 
  • A passionate opinion: No check possible. 

Approach (Diplomacy, Deception, or Intimidation) 
  • Right: -1 Difficulty Tier 
  • Neither: No change 
  • Wrong: +1 Difficulty Tier 

Disposition: 
  • Helpful: -2 Difficulty Tiers 
  • Friendly: -1 Difficulty Tiers 
  • Indifferent:  No change 
  • Unfriendly: +1 Difficulty Tier 
  • Hostile: +2 Difficulty Tiers 

Biases:
  • Appeal to: -1 Difficulty Tier 
  • Represent: +1 Difficulty Tier 

Aid from Friends: 
Up to three other people may attempt to aid in a persuasion attempt, chiming in with any skill they feel is relevant (even if it isn't normally a social skill.) Depending on the target's interests, some of these skill rolls may have no effect (unless they critically fail, in which case it is still a negative effect.) Aid still only provides a bonus to the primary persuader, it doesn't reduce or increase the difficulty tier.
