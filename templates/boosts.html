Boosts


Boost further enhance the usability of your effects, improving upon their existing perimeters, removing barriers from them, or giving them entirely new options.

Boosts usually increase the Mod of the ability, but on rare occasions may either increase the Base or be their own new Base effect.

Boosts here are ranked from the lowest possible Mod to the highest.

Tier Mod +1

Alternate Linked Skill [Mod +8]



Available to: Any effect with an existing linked skill

You can substitute a different skill for the linked skill on this effect.

Consumable Item [Mod +0 through +6]


Available To: Any activated effect without an unlimited duration.

[Mod +0]



Your effect is only delivered via consumable items. By default, creating the item is a free action (unless you have other activation limits), you can only have 1 item in existence at a time, and it is potent until your next daily preparations. The item is an item, meaning passing it from one character to another still takes an interact action, and it follows all stowing and retrieving rules.
The item is 1L bulk. Using the consumable also takes 1 interact action, and the effect triggers when you do, including lasting for its normal duration.

If your effect has an instantaneous duration, then by activating the item the creature instantly receives the benefit.



If your effect is a special attack, they are usually bullets, bombs, magic wands, or poisons. Any creature using these to attack are considered specialized in them.
The way activations work for these is usually whatever makes the most sense for the item, but by default if you activate the item, you use the attack immediately. If you modify the number of actions the attack takes, this modification applies to the activator of the item, not your activation of it. To modify how long it takes you to make the item, use the "Slower activation" limit instead of the rules in Special Attack.


Modifications:

  • You can have 2 items in existence at a time - though you still must still activated this effect as normal, creating a single item, including paying any costs or following any rules (Mod +2)
  • You can have 3 items in existence at a time - though you still must still activated this effect as normal, creating a single item, including paying any costs or following any rules (Mod +4)
  • Shorter Potency (Encounter: Until end of your next turn, Exploration: One Hour.)
    (Mod -2)

Note: You can apply activation boosts and limits twice with a consumable item, specifying which apply to your creating the item and which apply to the user activating it.



Difficult to Identify [Mod +1 through +5]



Any ability that would identify the nature of your effect has reduced effect.
Note that some abilities may still be able to affect your effect (even dispel it) without identifying it.

  • The penalty is -2 (Mod +1)
  • The check has disadvantage (Mod +3)
  • Your ability is impossible to identify (Mod +5)

Includes Unknown Information [Mod +2]



(Note: This boost has a strong intended spirit, and can be broken if applied incorrectly. Work with your GM on making sure your effect makes sense to use this on.)


Your effect can impact specifics that you don't actually know, but this cannot give you knowledge you don't have. For example, if you wanted to build an ability that uses an Alter Matter effect to alter the words within a book to specifically find and replace one word for another word, without this boost you would need to manually go through the book and see each instance in order to effect it. This boost allows the power to 'magically know and find' those instances for you.



(Warning!) The spirit of this boost is for hand waving magical effects that otherwise would be impossible without the character explicitly taking the time to learn information or see minutiae. This boost cannot be used in a fashion designed to supply the character special insight they didn't have, or to allow for tactical gain that breaks line of effect.

Increased Area Category [Base 4 and greater]



Available to: Any effect. Some effects are worded such that they only affect you, so don't need area. If you want these to affect others, you'll need to buy that boost first, then you can increase its area with this one.

Remember also that all effects require line of effect and sight, regardless of are, unless otherwise specified.

Adding Area: You can add Area to an effect that otherwise doesn't have it (typically Special Attacks), giving them one of the areas from Area Category 1. Doing so costs Base +4.

Increasing Area: You can increase an area of effect at the cost of:

  • One category shift: Mod +8
  • Second category shift: An additional Mod +8
  • Third category shift: Base +4
  • Fourth category shift: An additional Mod +8
  • Fifth category shift: An additional Base +4


You must pay for each shift, so all five category shift increases would cost Base +8, Mod +24. No effect's area can be increased by more than five categories.

(See: Range, Area, Duration)

If the effect's Area is represented on the chart, then pick the nearest closest Area (rounded down).

Increased Duration Category [Mod +4 and greater]



Available to: any effect with a non-instantaneous duration.

(See: Range, Area, Duration)



Per each category increase:
Mod +4

No ability that modifies or effects creatures can be increased beyond "24 hours", unless otherwise noted in the effect.


Increase your duration by one category.

If the effect's Duration is represented on the chart, then pick the nearest closest Duration (rounded down).

Increased Range Category [Mod +4 and greater]


Available to: any non special attack effect with a range. Some effects are worded such that they only affect you, so don't need range. If you want these to affect others, you'll need to buy that boost first, then you can increase its range with this one.



(See: Range, Area, Duration)

Per each category increase: Mod +4

Increase your range by one category. Remember also that all effects require line of effect and sight, regardless of range, unless otherwise specified.

If the effect's range is represented on the chart, then pick the nearest closest range (rounded down).

Provides Cover [Mod +2]



Available to: An activated effect with a duration of at least one round, and an area of at least one square.

[Mod +2]

Your area provides cover, as if it were a solid object - even if it isn't manifested as a solid object. This cover is only applicable to creatures on either side of it, even if one of them is within the area, but not if both are.

Underwater Effectiveness [Mod +1]



[Mod +1]



This effect can be used normally underwater, despite what underwater rules my effect it. Typically, this overcomes one or more of the following:

  • Effects the fire trait cannot normally be used underwater
  • Creatures normally have 5 fire resistance against fire effects
  • Ranged Slashing or Bludgeoning effects cannot function normally

Tier Mod +2

Add Password [Mod +4]



Available to: any non-attack effect with a duration longer than 1 round.



You can embed the ability for creature of your choice to temporarily disable or bypass your effect by speaking a password. This boost doesn't automatically inform them of the password, you will have to do that before or after you use the effect.


Beneficial To Others [Mod +8]



Available to: any effect that normally only benefits you.

This boost lets you grant the benefits of an effect that otherwise only affects you to others.


  • Non Attacks: If your ability is a persistent (always on) effect, it defaults as only affecting you, but at the beginning of any round you may choose to have it affect a single creature you can touch
    instead (this doesn't take any actions, unless you have that limit.) If your ability requires activation, you may choose another target than yourself that you can touch when activating the ability. You can increase your range beyond touch with the "Increased Range" boost.


  • Special Attacks: If you wish to grant creatures your instantaneous special attacks, this boost allows you to transfer your ability to attack to another creature that you can touch. They must use their own attack skill to attack with it. Only one creature may attack with your special ability each round, even if you modify this boost to include multiple targets. If your special attack specifically has the Adds to Strike, "Activated" version, then follow the rules above for Non Attacks. You can increase your range beyond touch with the "Increased Range" boost.

If you apply an area, the default assumption is the ability is beneficial to all qualifying creatures in the area. You'll need to pick "Select Targets" options to avoid this.

Modifications to this Boost:

  • You can never affect yourself, only others (Mod -8)

Concealable [Mod +10]



Available to: effects that require some type of action or activation, and cannot be applied to attacks that deal damage

You can attempt to conceal your usage of an effect.

When activating your effect, you can attempt either a deception check (if it has the verbal component) or a stealth check (If it has the somatic component, or neither component), or both (if it has both the verbal and somatic components) against any observers' perception DC.




If you succeed, then you weave your actions into appropriate actions for the situation. You may, for example, weave a charm spell into a normal conversation - which may be critical unless you rapidly speed up activation times.

This ability hides only your actions and manifestations, not your feat's effects, so an observer might still see a ray streak out from you or see you vanish into thin air. Further, some abilities may exist that specifically counter this concealment. It only applies to visual and audio elements. A creature that could see magic, for example, still may see magic being worked around you.

Modification:

  • Instead of requiring a check, the effect is automatically hidden unless observing creatures can obviously guess via context clues, then they can make an appropriate Recall Knowledge check. (Base +4)
  • Instead of completely concealing your effect entirely, you pass it off as a harmless (neither beneficial nor harmful) effect, but otherwise must still follow the base rules of attempting to do so (observers can still attempt a Recall Knowledge check to determine what it really was). An example would be activating a charm effect that you can pass off as activating the ability to detect truths. (Mod -2)

Difficult to Counter, Suppress, or Disrupt [Mod +4 through Mod +10 ]



Any ability that would attempt to counteract, suppress or disrupt your ability has reduced effect.

  • The penalty is -2 (Mod +4)
  • The check has disadvantage (Mod +6)
  • Your ability is impossible to counteract or disrupt (Mod +10)

Faster Activation [Mod +8 - +16]

Available to: Any effect that has the Activation: Duration or Activation: Instantaneous Keyword (this doesn't apply to selecting the Activation Limit)

You can activate this effect more quickly than normal.

  • Faster Activation (Mod +8)


    • In Encounter mode, activation takes only 1 action or a reaction (Use the Situational Limit to set Trigger, including the reduction in cost)
    • In Exploration mode, activation only takes 5 minutes
    • In Downtime mode, activation only takes 1.5 hours
  • Much Faster Activation (Mod +12)


    • In Encounter mode, activation takes a free action (usable once per round, only 2 abilities with this boost can be activated per round as a free action)
    • In Exploration mode, activation only takes 1 minute
    • In Downtime mode, activation only takes 15 minutes
  • Incredibly Faster Activation (Mod +16)


    • In Encounter mode, activation takes a triggered free action (usable once per round, only 2 abilities with this boost can be activated per round as a free action)
    • In Exploration mode, activation only takes 6 seconds (3 actions)
    • In Downtime mode, activation only takes 1 minute

Penetrating [Mod +3 and greater]



Available to: Special Attacks



[Mod +3 per point of hardness or resistance]

For your special attack, reduce the hardness or damage resistance of an object or creature that you damage by the amount you pay for. The hardness or resistance may still be high enough to reduce your damage to 0.

Unusual Defense Type [Mod +8]



Available to: Any effect that provides a defense type.



You can select a different defense type than what is normally required.

Tier Mod +3

Font [Mod +12]



Available to: effects with the trait "Activated: Instantaneous".



Your effect now has the "Activated: Duration" trait instead. Instead of the effect immediately triggering after activation, it can instead be used once during the duration (use the Exploration duration, even if you use the ability in encounter mode). Your effect is also centered on a single target within range, which could be either yourself, an object, or a single square (you must pick the type of object that the effect can be centered on when you build the ability.) In order for an applicable creature (including yourself) to gain the benefit of the effect, they must spend an action to interact with the font. As usual, if you select an area of effect, the font can be an area, and when activated applies to all creatures in that area.

The effect's range cannot be increased, nor can an effect "fire out" from the font. It effects either the creature who interacted with it, or all creatures in the area if it has one.

Selectable Targets within Area [Mod +16 or greater]



Available to: effect with an Area of Effect.

You can select which targets are hit within your area of effect.

  • You can either select two targets within your area (your area hits no other targets), or deselect one target (pick which when selecting this boost)
    [Mod +16]


    • Per additional target: [Mod +6]


Damages Matter [Mod +12 to Mod +16]



Applicable to: Special Attacks

By default, attacks will do superficial damage most of the time, and substantial damage on occasion to objects in the environment. Typically this will be purely adjudicated based on the situation, but you may find that no matter how much damage you do with your club, the GM won't let you sever a rope with it. They may also decide that a torch, no matter how good you are with it, won't eventually burn through a stone wall. This is how the rules are intended to work. This boost, however, allows you to supersede that organic logic. Attacks with this boost can deal damage to matter using the standard rules of hardness and broken and destroyed thresholds no matter how illogical that seems.






Modification:

  • Can be done covertly and in a controlled fashion, possibly allowing you to attack matter quietly and discreetly. (Mod +4)
    (Linked skill: Thievery)

Movable Area ]Mod +12 to Mod +18]



Available to: Any effect with an area and a duration longer than instantaneous.



Your area of effect can be moved. There are two version of this boost:

  • Mental:
    You can move the ongoing effect mentally. This requires 1 action with the concentration trait, and can move a default distance of 30 feet. Any of those things can be improved with boosts, but the maximum distance an effect can move with a single action is 120 feet. By default, this effect "teleports" from one location to the other, and isn't "Drug through" the spaces it moves through, unless the effect has no affect on creatures. (Mod +12)
  • Attached: When you activate this effect, it begins attached to an unattended object, a willing creature, or an object attended by a willing creature. When the creature or object moves, the effect stops working until the creature/object stops moving again, unless the effect has no affect on creatures. (Mod +14)


Modifications:

  • Drags through spaces: This effect affects all the spaces it moves through if applicable (meaning, if simply touching the area is enough to trigger its outcome, then creatures or objects in the intervening space between where your area started and where it moves are affected.) (Mod +6)


Special Note: If you want your area to "Pull creatures along with it" - then you'll need to select the Special Attack feature "forced movement." The maximum distance a creature can be moved with it is equal to the lesser of that feature, or this one. However, you can choose to stop moving an area at any time, even after you learn that a creature isn't going to be forced to move along with it as far as you thought (because of a successful defense, for example.)

Tier Mod +4

Trap [Mod +14]


Available to: Special Attacks or activated non-attacks.

You set this effect into the environment, where it lays dormant until triggered. This is similar to Contingency, but rather than a modification you apply to any effect (like Contingency suggests) this is specific to the effect this modification is boosting. You must choose the "Situational" limit to set the triggering condition, and once triggered, the effect immediately occurs.


If the effect is a non-area special attack, the target is automatically the individual who triggered the trap. If not an attack, your Trap is only applicable to effects that require an activation of some kind.


Traps can be detected and disarmed, by default. If a creature is seeking, you may be asked to make a check against their Perception DC to see if they detect the trap, and against their disable device action (thievery) DC to see if they disarm it.

Trigger Area: The effect area is set when you build the effect, but this is how big of an area you can designate as the 'trapped' space when it comes to setting off your trap.

  • A single 1B or less item. (default)
  • A 5x5 foot square (Mod +1)
  • A 5 foot burst (Mod +2)
  • A 10 foot burst (Mod +3)
  • A 15 foot burst
    (Mod +4)
  • A 20 foot burst
    (Mod +5)


Persistence:

  • Your Trap lasts 8 hours (base)
  • Your Trap lasts 24 hours (Mod +2)
  • Your Trap lasts 1 week
    (Mod +4)
  • Your Trap lasts indefinitely (Mod +8)


Iterations of this Trap you can have active at a time:

  • 1 Trap (base)
  • 2 Traps (Mod +4)
  • 3 Traps (Mod +8)


Special Modifiers:

  • Your traps are -1 to detect or disarm (Mod +2)
  • Your traps are -2 to detect or disarm (Mod +4)
  • Your traps have disadvantage to detect or disarm (Mod +8)
  • The check to disable your trap can be a specific skill you choose
    from this list, in addition to Thievery:
    Arcana, Religion or Nature. (Mod -2)
  • The check to disable your trap must be a specific skill you choose from this list, instead of Thievery:
    Arcana, Religion or Nature. (Mod +4)

  • Your trap can be placed underwater or in the air, in a static location. The burst is 3 dimensional in this case (Mod +4)
  • You can recover and move your trap. After you've placed it, as long as it hasn't been triggered, you can pick it up and store it, either physically or metaphysically. Doing so recovers any resources or resets any frequency limits you used laying it.
    (Mod +4)
  • Instead of a triggering condition, the trap automatically fires after a timed duration (up to 24 hours) chosen at the creation of the ability (Mod +0)


    • You can instead choose the timing when you activate the ability (Mod +4)
