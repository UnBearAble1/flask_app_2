Elite Anchorite of Talos


Threat: 7

These religious recluses are granted spellcasting power by Talos, the god of storms. Their human ancestors bred with orcs, and now all anchorites of Talos are half-orcs.

Recall Knowledge: Religion

Medium, Half-Orc, Humanoid, Controller, Elite
Perception +10
Languages Common
Skills Diplomacy +6, Medicine +10, Religion +11, Society +8, Survival +8
Str +3, Dex +3, Con +1, Int +0, Wis +4, Cha +2
AC 19; Fort +8 , Ref +10 , Will +12
HP 42 (Begins with 50 temporary hit points); Immunity: Electricity Damage
Speed 30 feet

Features:

  • Electric Skin: Anyone who deals damage to the Anchorite or one of it's Mirror Images in melee combat takes 2d12 electricity damage
  • Second Chance: Every third round of combat the Anchorite's barrier (it's Temp HP) is restored 15.

Reaction:

  • (Free action) Reactive Mirror


    • Trigger: The Anchorite rolls initiative
    • Effect: A contingent Mirror Image (master tier) is instantly cast on the Anchorite.
  • Clever Counterspell:


    • Trigger: A creature within sight casts a spell on the Primal or Arcane spell list
    • Effect: The Anchorite rolls a Cast a Spell check (+11) with the same DC, and uses that roll a counteract roll for the spell.

Actions:

  • Lightning Bolt (3 x a day, innate)
  • Gust of Wind (3 x a day, 1 Action, innate)
  • Sudden Bolt (5 x a day, innate)
  • Shocking Grasp (At Will)

Elite Free Actions:

  • Lightning Morph: The Anchorite transforms into a bolt of lightning, and rips through targets as per Chain Lightning - if the spell ends before all targets are struck, he appears next to the target who ended the effect. Otherwise, the bolt double back to any point within range and he appears there.
  • Damning Fog:
    The Anchorite summons a cloud of mist, as per Obscuring Mist, though he can see through it. Any creature that starts their turn in the mist must make a Willpower save DC 21

    • Crit Success: No effect
    • Success: Doomed 1
    • Failure: Doomed 2
    • Critical Failure: Doomed 3
  • Wall of Storms: The Anchorite summons a wall (up to 50 feet high, and 100 feet wide, and 2 feet thick) The wall cannot be seen through, and creatures who move through the wall immediately take 4d12 points of electricity damage.
