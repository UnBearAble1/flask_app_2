Bulk and Lifting


Your character's Bulk Limit determines how much weight they can comfortably carry. If they're carrying a total amount of Bulk Limit they are encumbered. A character can't carry a total amount of Bulk that exceeds double their Bulk Limit. The Bulk your character is carrying equals the sum of all of their items; keep in mind that 10 light items make up 1 Bulk.

A character's Bulk Limit is 5 + their STR modifier, with a divider or multiplier based on their size:

  • Tiny: divided by 4 (round up to the nearest 10th, decimals allowing L weight items)
  • Small: divided by 2 (round up to the nearest 10th, decimals allowing L weight items)
  • Medium: multiplied by 1
  • Large: multiplied by 4
  • Huge: multiplied by 10
  • Gargantuan: multiplied by 20

Example:
A tiny creature with a STR mod of +4 has a Bulk Limit of 2.3 (5+4 = 9 base, divided by 4 for size is 2.25, rounded up- they could carry 2B and 3L worth of items), where as a huge creature with a STR mod of +2 has a Bulk Limit of 28 (5+2 = 7 base, multiplied by 4 for size).

The same multiplier above are also used for appropriate sized equipment. A pixie's longsword would be 1/4th the bulk of a human's (3L) where as a huge plague giant's longsword would be 10x the bulk (10B).

Maximum LIft:

A creature can lift 15kg per point of Bulk Limit they have.

Example:
human with a +1 strength (above average) can lift 90 kg (198 lbs,) where as an Adult Green Dragon with a strength of +6 can lift 1,650 kg (3,670 lbs.)

Using the Power Lift Athletics action can potentially double this number.
