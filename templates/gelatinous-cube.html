Gelatinous Cube


Threat: 6

Found underground or in dungeons, these quivering cubes of slime continuously scour their domain for food. The acid in their bodies is weak enough that many gelatinous cubes still contain the gear of their victims, as they're unable to break them down.

Recall Knowledge - Ooze (Occultism)

N, Large, Mindless, Ooze, Brute
Perception +2; motion sense 60 feet, no vision
- Motion Sense A gelatinous cube can sense nearby motion through vibration and air movement.
Skills Athletics +8 (+13 to Shove)
Str +4, Dex -5, Con +5, Int -5, Wis +0, Cha -5
AC 19; Fort +11 , Ref +7 , Will +9
HP 100; Immunities acid, critical hits, mental, precision, unconscious, visual; Resistances electricity 5
Speed 15 feet

Features:

  • Transparent: A gelatinous cube is so clear that it's difficult to spot. A successful DC 21 Perception check is required to notice a stationary cube, and a creature must be Searching to attempt this check. A creature that walks into the cube is automatically Engulfed (this usually causes the GM to call for initiative).
  • Weak Acid; A gelatinous cube's acid damages only organic material--not metal, stone, or other inorganic substances.

Actions:

  • Face: Single Action cube; +8 [+3/-2], Damage 2d8+6 acid plus paralysis
  • Engulf; Two Actions DC 190, 2d10 acid, Escape DC 16, Rupture 7. A creature Engulfed by the gelatinous cube must also attempt a saving throw against paralysis.

    • Paralysis (incapacitation) A creature Engulfed by the cube or hit by its attack is paralyzed unless it succeeds at a DC 19 Fortitude save. A victim can attempt a new save to recover at the end of each of its turns.
