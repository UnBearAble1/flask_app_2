Spirit Instinct


8 Points
Warrior; Rage
Instinct-style; Prerequisites: Rage
Limit: You can only have one instinct-style feat.

Whether you are emotionally sensitive to the spirits around you; worship ancestors or apparitions; or are haunted by the specter of an ancestor, relative, friend, or foe, your rage takes the form of a spiritual possession.

Spirit Rage: While raging, you can increase the additional damage from Rage from 2 to 3 and change its damage type to negative or positive, instead of the damage type for your weapon or unarmed attack (choose each time you Rage). If you choose to deal negative or positive damage, your weapon or unarmed attack gains the effects of the ghost touch property, which makes it more effective against incorporeal creatures, and your Rage action gains the divine and necromancy traits, plus negative or positive, as appropriate.



Anethema: Disrespecting corpses or spirits is anathema to your instinct; defending yourself against undead creatures is not. Whenever you perform such acts, you lose the instinct's abilities and any feats that list your instinct as a prerequisite until you spend 1 day of downtime re-centering yourself, though you keep all of your other barbarian abilities.



Raging Resistance: You resist your Con Modifier in piercing and negative damage, as well as damage dealt by the attacks and abilities of undead creatures, regardless of the damage type.

Special: You can only benefit from one Instinct type per rage
