The Situational Limit


This limit is going to be a cornerstone of most abilities, allowing some truly customizable limitations and giving many abilities a great deal of nuance and flavor. It also is one of those complex ones, requiring some work between the player and the GM to make sure the limit fits within the context of the game, and is in the right price category.



A situational limit is one that specifies under what conditions your ability can be used. If those conditions are not met, the ability can not be used.



There are three flavors of this limit: Triggers, often used for reactions, Tactical Encounter Requirements, often used for non-reaction or non-triggered-free action combat abilities, and Exploration Requirements, often used for everything else. If you wish to apply one of the three categories to an effect that otherwise should fall outside of its purview, you'll need to work with the GM -- the point reductions are balanced specifically for their intended purposes.

When selecting limits, you'll want to pick one that makes sense. If you look at the examples, and don't take them within their intended context, some may be a little too easy.


There are also two modifications to this limit you can explore:

Resettable Situations

Some situational limits may be appropriate, but too restrictive. Many of us have played the Ranger in the world's most popular roleplaying game, only to find ourselves being unable to use half of our abilities most of the time because of restrictions on target or environment.

If you reduce the restriction an entire two categories (for example "Usually Easy Setup" to "Very Easy Setup") you can add the following modification: "Each day, as part of your daily preparations, you can swap out the condition of this limit to a similar one."

For example, if you have an ability that only works on Undead (typically Difficult Setup / Limited Effectiveness (-35%) [Tier A]), you could add the ability to swap out the type of creature for another type each day, but the point value reduces down to (Easy Setup / Slightly limited Effectiveness: (-15%))

Incredibly Situational

For every three Situation Limits of the same or greater value, you can increase the Base or Tier Mod of the lowest Situational Limit by +1.

Triggers.

Triggers are designed for reactions or triggered-free actions.

To make a setup a trigger for a reaction or a triggered free action, it must be "Instantaneous." This list has the most common types of these, though you could possibly use the Tactical Requirements list for these types of actions, but adding the language that your most recent action was to perform the action, or those setups that require actions on other creatures' parts, such as allies or the target, assume the action was performed since your last turn.

No matter how rare the specifics are, if there is only one possible situation in which your effect would need to fire, that trigger can be no higher than "Easy Setup."
For example, gaining sonic resistance against a single attack would obviously require you to be taking sonic damage for it to even matter - so even if "Taking sonic damage" is listed as "Usually Easy" - because it's the only reason to use the power, it is downgraded to "easy Setup."

Trigger: Very Easy Setup (Limit -2)


These triggers are almost always going to happen organically, usually even each and every turn.




Examples:

  • Target takes damage
  • Target successfully hits one of your allies
  • Target successfully damages one of your allies
  • Target uses a move action
  • You perform an easy action, such as "Raise a Shield"
  • You perform a specific move action (stride, step, leap, etc)
  • You take physical damage (bludgeoning, slashing, or piercing)
  • An ally performs an easy action, such as "Raise a Shield"
  • An ally performs a specific move action (stride, step, leap, etc)
  • Initiative is rolled
  • The trigger is magical
  • The trigger is non-magical

Trigger: Easy Setup (Limit -6)

These triggers may require a bit of positioning or effort to make happen, but nothing you can't accomplish with a single action. Or they are very likely to occur each round.

Examples:

  • Target becomes Flat Footed
  • Target becomes Marked
  • Target becomes Flanked
  • Target becomes Frightened
  • Target is missed twice this round
  • Target misses any creature
  • Target gains a serious wound
  • You are knocked prone
  • You take a fire, cold, poison, negative, or alignment damage
  • Is a non-spell/alchemy Augmentation effect
  • You become Marked
  • You become Flat Footed
  • You become Flanked
  • You successfully hit the target with a Strike

Trigger: Usually Easy Setup (Limit -10)

These won't happen every turn unless you are using a special ability to make them happen, or things possibly occur organically (but the chance of that happening would be around 30%)



Examples:

  • Any creature within 60 feet gains a specific condition
  • Target is knocked Prone
  • Target uses a manipulate action
  • Target critically misses any creature
  • Target critically hits any creature
  • Target is critically hit by anyone
  • Target is damaged twice this round
  • Target is missed three times this round
  • Target becomes Bloodied
  • Target gains a grievous wound
  • Target successfully hits you with any combat maneuver (trip, grapple, shove, or disarm)
  • Is a spell/alchemy augmentation effect
  • You take lightning, acid, force, sonic or mental damage
  • You gain any specific other condition besides Prone, Marked, or Flat Footed
  • You become Bloodied

  • You gain a serious wound
  • An ally gains a serious wound
  • You start taking any persistent damage
  • You successfully hit with a specific other Special attack
  • You successfully hit with a specific special activity (such as Escape, Feint or Demoralize)
  • You fail an attack roll with a specific special activity (such as Escape, Feint or Demoralize), or a non-combat activity.
  • You leave a stance

Trigger: Difficult Setup (Limit -14)

These triggers require more careful set up, or a bit of luck, naturally occurring about 15% of the time or so.




Examples:

  • Target gains any specific other condition besides Prone, Marked, Frightened or Flat Footed
  • You shield block the target
  • Target critically misses you
  • Target critically hits you
  • Target is critically hit by you
  • Target is critically hit by an ally
  • Target successfully uses a specific special activity against you (such as Escape, Feint or Demoralize)
  • You reduce a target to 0 hit points
  • An ally reduces a target to 0 hit points
  • An ally gains a grievous wound

  • You critically succeed at specific special activity (such as Escape, Feint or Demoralize)
  • You critically fail an attack roll with a specific special activity (such as Escape, Feint or Demoralize) or a non-combat activity.
  • Over the past two rounds, you have hit successfully twice with a specific (or two specific) other Special Attack feats that require rolls and are Tier C or greater.

Trigger: Very Difficult Setup (Limit -18)



These triggers aren't likely to happen organically (probably 5% chance in any given round), but you could possibly work to set them up with a great deal of effort or timing.




Examples:

  • Target was damaged three times this round
  • You are attacked by a creature that you haven't yet acted hostile toward.
  • You shield block and your shield is destroyed
  • You gain a Grievous wound
  • Over the past two rounds, you have hit successfully twice with a specific (or two specific) other Special Attack feats that require rolls and are Tier B or greater.
  • An Ally is reduced to 0 hit points

Trigger: Extremely Difficult Setup (Limit -22)

These are once in a blue moon type triggers.


Examples:

  • You take a specific type of persistent damage for the first time this combat, given to you by an enemy creature or hazard
  • Over the past two rounds, you have hit successfully twice with a specific (or two specific) other Special Attack feats that require rolls and are Tier A or greater.



Tactical Encounter Requirements.



Tactical Encounter requirements are mostly for special attacks, defenses or any other combat maneuver that can only be used under certain conditions that typically occur in combat. If your special ability is normally used in encounter mode, but isn't a reaction or a triggered reaction, this is the Situational Limit category for you.

Tactical Encounter: Easy Setup (Limit -6)


these are almost always going to happen organically, usually even each and every turn.




Examples:

  • Target is Flat Footed
  • Target is Marked
  • Target is Flanked
  • Target was missed twice since your last turn
  • Target has a serious wound
  • target is at full hit points
  • Target has taken damage since your last turn

  • Target successfully hit one of your allies since your last turn
  • Target is Bloodied
  • Target is larger than you (and you are Tiny or Small)
  • You have your shield raised
  • You are prone
  • You are Marked
  • You are Flat Footed
  • You are Flanked
  • You are within reach of 2 targets
  • You are adjacent to an ally
  • You and two allies are within reach of a target
  • You performed a specific move action (stride, step, leap, etc) this turn
  • You are in a specific stance
  • You have successfully hit the target with a Strike this turn
  • Creature is taller than you (and you are small)
  • You have the high ground

Tactical Encounter: Usually Easy Setup (Limit -10)



These may require a bit of positioning or effort to make happen, but nothing you can't accomplish with a single action. Or they are likely to occur each round.




Examples:

  • Target is Prone
  • Target critically missed any creature since your last turn
  • Target critically hit any creature since your last turn
  • Target was damaged twice since your last turn
  • Target was missed three times since your last turn
  • Target is Large or larger
  • Target has a grievous wound
  • Target is in difficult terrain (including that which you create)
  • You have any specific other condition besides Prone, Marked, or Flat Footed
  • You are within reach of 3 targets
  • You are Bloodied

  • You are Hidden
  • You have a serious wound
  • You are taking any persistent damage
  • You are adjacent to two allies
  • You successfully hit with a specific other Special attack since your last turn
  • You successfully hit with a specific special activity (such as Escape, Feint or Demoralize) this turn
  • You are in a specific stance, and only one other feat can have this limit
  • Ally must Aid your action (From any range in the combat)
    since your last turn
  • You must be adjacent to a designated object
  • You have the low ground

Tactical Encounter: Difficult Setup (Limit -14)



These won't happen every turn unless you are using a special ability to make them happen, or things possibly occur organically (but the chance of that happening would be around 15%)




Examples:

  • Target has Any specific other condition besides Prone, Marked, or Flat Footed
  • You shield blocked the target since your last turn
  • Target critically missed you since your last turn
  • Target critically hit you
    since your last turn
  • Target was critically hit by you on your last action
  • Target was critically hit by an ally since your last turn
  • Target is in difficult terrain that you didn't create
  • You reduce a target to 0 hit points since your last turn
  • An ally reduces a target to 0 hit points since your last turn
  • You critically succeed at specific special activity (such as Escape, Feint or Demoralize) this turn
  • Ally must Aid your action (within melee reach of either you or target)
  • You have more than three onlookers who are not engaged in the activity (such as battle) this effect is used for.
  • Over the past two rounds, you have hit successfully twice with a specific (or two specific) other Special Attack feats that require rolls and are Tier B or greater.

Tactical Encounter: Very Difficult Setup (Limit -18)



These require more careful set up, or a bit of luck, naturally occurring about 5% of the time or so.




Examples:

  • You are Undetected
  • Target was damaged three times since the end of your last turn
  • You shield blocked and your shield was destroyed since your last turn
  • You received a Grievous wound within the last two rounds, and have not attacked since you received it
  • Over the past two rounds, you have hit successfully twice with a specific (or two specific) other Special Attack feats that require rolls and are Tier A or greater.
  • An Ally was reduced to 0 hit points since your last turn

Examples:

  • You are took a specific type of persistent damage for the first time this combat, given to you by an enemy creature or hazard
  • Over the past two rounds, you have hit successfully twice with a specific (or two specific) other Special Attack feats that require rolls and are Tier S.
  • Target must be dying


Exploration Requirements.




Exploration requirements are basically "everything else." They are largely situations set up for exploration effects.



For most exploration requirements, assume you can only use the ability once per new instances of meeting the requirement, not that you can continuously spam it throughout.

Exploration: Very Easy Setup / Almost Unlimited Effectiveness (Limit -2)

These setups are almost always happening.



Example:

  • Only usable during your Daily Preparations or during your long rest (typically this is tacked on to usable only once per day)

Exploration: Easy Setup / Slightly Limited Effectiveness (Limit -6)

These setups are common, but not always there. They may require a bit of set up.

Examples:

  • You are in a specific environmental terrain common to the campaign setting: aquatic, arctic, desert, forest, mountain, plains, sky, swamp, urban or underground (ask GM what environments apply)
  • Requires a specific object, bigger than yourself, to interact with, such as a bonfire, a body of water, or a tree, specified when you select this.
  • Only related to using a particular imprecise or vague sense

Exploration: Usually Easy Setup / Partially limited Effectiveness (Limit -10)

These are only applicable some of the time.



Examples:

  • Can only use when interacting with a specific tradition of magic (not that you are using)
  • Can only use when interacting a specific sphere of magic (not that you are using)
  • Limited to concealment from any/all of smoke, ash, fog, or mist
  • Limited to a specific type of creature common to the campaign world (undead, demon/devil, dragon, etc), work with your GM to see what is appropriate.
  • Limited to Hazards and Traps
  • Only possible when in darkness

Exploration: Difficult Setup / Limited Effectiveness (Limit -14)

These situations rarely occur.

Examples:

  • You are in a specific environmental terrain uncommon to the campaign setting: aquatic, arctic, desert, forest, mountain, plains, sky, swamp, urban or underground (ask GM what environments apply)
  • Limited to a specific type of creature uncommon to the campaign world (undead, demon/devil, dragon, etc), work with your GM to see what is appropriate.
  • Limited to a specific elemental or energy type
  • You are following the expert
  • Others are following you as the expert
  • Limited to concealment from one: smoke, ash, fog, or mist


Exploration: Very Difficult Setup / Very limited Effectiveness (Limit -18)

This almost never happen, or are extremely hard to set up.


  • You have to have some of the target's body (blood, hair, etc) or a very personal object of the targets (assumes the target is typically an enemy or a stranger, this limit is not applicable for effects that target allies)
  • Only possible during a specific moon phase

Exploration: Extremely Difficult Setup / Extremely limited Effectiveness (Limit -22)

These are very, very rare occurrences.



Examples:

  • Only possible during a specific day of the year
  • You have to be bathed in the fresh blood of a powerful creature or foe
