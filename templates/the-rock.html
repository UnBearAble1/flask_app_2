The Rock


The Rock is an arena pugilist turned adventurer.

Base: Warrior
HP: 100


SW: 30

GW: 40
Languages: Common, Giant
Specialties: Lore: Arena Sports, Athletics, Might, Coordination, Empathy, Diplomacy, Intimidation, Society

Armor: Light. DR 1.

  • Fortified: The DR applies to Slashing, Piercing, and Bludgeoning
  • Comfort: You can rest in this armor.
  • Concealable: This armor is fully concealed in clothing

Weapons: Brawling Gear.
1d6 bludgeoning, 1 handed.

  • Free Hand:
    Hands count as empty.
  • Grapple: +3 to Grapple Checks


Combat Moves:


  • Wrestling Slams: (2 Actions) [Nonlethal] The Rock suplexes, slams, DDTs, or pile-drives his foe. Make an attack vs Guard Defense.


    • Success: 2d10+10 damage, and target is prone.
    • Critical Success: 6d6+10 damage, and target is prone.



  • Focus Spell: The People's Eyebrow (Intimidating) [Open]: The Rock raises an eyebrow and focuses his menace on a group of jabronis. He can demoralize 3 targets. When he does so, this Demoralize
    loses the auditory trait and gains the visual trait, and he doesn't take a penalty if it doesn't understand his language. Additionally, any creature demoralized this way is subject to a secondary Willpower attack:

    • Success: The first round doesn't count toward the depreciation of their frightened condition.
    • Critical Success: As success, but also deals 2d6 mental damage.
(Creatures demoralized this way can spend an action and contest the Rock with their athletics on their turn, removing the frightened condition on a success.)




  • Finishing Move: [2 Actions] (Cost to Activate: 1 Hero Point) The Rock hits a Rock Bottom to a standing foe, or a People's Elbow to a prone one. Whether he succeeds or not, he is Flat-Footed for the rest of the round, and for two rounds afterwards. He can only use this move once per week, though if he takes a Grievous Wound, as long as this is the first attack he makes afterwards, he can ignore this restriction (or not count toward it.) Make an attack vs Fortitude.

    • Success [Ghost Touch, Magical, Death]: 12d6+10 damage.
    • Critical Success: The target dies.



  • Counter Grapple [Manipulate, Flourish]: (Reaction). If a target within reach misses any creature, the Rock may attempt to grapple them.



  • Focus Spell: It doesn't MATTER what you're saying! (Reaction) The Rock interrupts a speech by informing the target that it does not, in fact, matter what they are saying. Trigger: A creature within 120 feet uses an effect with the auditory trait or a verbal component. Effect: The Rock makes a counteract check.

    • Success: The effect is suppressed for 1 minute, if this is longer than its remaining duration, it is dispelled.
    • Critical Success: it is dispelled regardless of the duration. If the creature has the ability to activate the effect again, the rock automatically rolls another counteract check. They can only try this once per round or until they successfully use the ability.



Other Combat Benefits:


  • Kip Up: When attempting to stand up, the Rock can make a Coordination check versus the Might or Coordination of all creatures within reach (one roll). On a success, standing does not provoke reaction attacks. On a failure, the Rock is -10 to speed for 2 rounds.



  • Grappling Master:
    The Rock can use combat maneuvers creatures three times larger than him, and reduces the penalties for size difference by 1 per size category.



  • Backstage Brawler:
    The Rock doesn't take penalties for attacking with improvised weapons, and gains critical specialization effects with improvised weapons (the GM will determine best fit for each).



  • Lethal, Nonlethal, Whatever:
    The rock doesn't take penalties for making weapons nonlethal, or for making his unarmed attacks lethal.







Non Combat Abilities:



  • Focus Spell: The People's Eyebrow (Charming) [Emotion, Visual]: The Rock raises an eyebrow and focuses his charisma on a target. For 2 actions (instead of a minute of conversation), he can Make an Impression. He cannot fail Intermediate (DC 5 or lower) challenges, but can still critically fail on a natural 1.



  • Promo Master: [Concentrate] By hyping himself up for 10 minutes, The Rock can enter a state of concentrated Diplomacy, which lasts for an hour. If he takes any damage in this state, he must make a DC 10 flat check or fall out of it. While in this state, If he critically fails a Make an Impression roll it counts as a failure instead.


    • He can also enter this state as a reaction to critically failing a Request roll, but the benefit only applies to Request attempts.



  • +5 Bulk Limits: The Rock can carry 15 bulk, and lift 496 lbs (possibly up to double with a Might check).



  • No Crowbar Needed: The Rock doesn't take any penalties for not having tools (crowbars, etc) when using Force Open
