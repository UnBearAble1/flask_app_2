Giant Moray Eel


Threat 5

Giant moray eels dwell in warm, tropical waters and build lairs in narrow, twisting caves made of coral. Their size, speed, and powerful bite make them dangerous to divers and fishermen. Giant moray eels have rubbery hides that secrete a layer of mucus, making them difficult to harm with some weapons.

Recall Knowledge - Animal (Nature)

N, Large, Animal, Aquatic, Lurker
Perception +9; low-light vision, scent (imprecise) 30 feet
Skills Acrobatics +7, Athletics +11, Stealth +7
Str +6, Dex +2, Con +3, Int -4, Wis +2, Cha -1
AC 18; Fort +6, Ref +10, Will +8
HP 60; Resistances bludgeoning 5, piercing 5
Speed 10 feet, swim 40 feet

Reaction:

  • Ambush:


    • Trigger A target creature passes within 20 feet of the giant moray eel's hiding place and has not detected the giant moray eel.
    • Effect The giant moray eel lunges out of its hiding place, Swims directly toward the triggering creature, and makes a jaws Strike against it. The target creature is flat-footed to this attack.
  • Sneak Attack: The Giant Moray Eel does an additional +2d6 dice damage to a flat-footed creature

Actions:

  • Jaws; Melee Single Action;
    +9 [+4/-1] (reach 10 feet), Damage 2d8+5 piercing plus Grab
  • Pharyngeal Jaws; One Action;


    • Requirements: The giant moray eel has a creature grabbed in its jaws.
    • Effect The giant moray eel uses its second set of jaws to pull the prey into its gullet. The eel deals 2d6+5 piercing damage to the grabbed creature and gains a +2 circumstance bonus to its Swallow Whole attempts and to the DC for the creature to Escape. This effect ends if the target Escapes or the giant moray eel Swallows it Whole.
  • Swallow Whole; Single Action (attack) Small, 2d6+6 bludgeoning, Rupture 12
