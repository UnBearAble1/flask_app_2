Orc Marauder


This build is really good at killing stuff with a big sword, has a high armor class, and constantly makes foes frightened (and keeps them that way) while also making them flat-footed. Expect more critical hits than usual, and for big damage.




Inspiration:
Male, Female

The Orc Marauder
Legendary Warrior, Expert Professional

STR +4, DEX +1, CON +3, INT -1, WIS +0, CHR +2
AC: 24

HP: 87
Speed: 30
Perception (expert):
+4

Fortitude (master):
+9

Reflex (expert, mighty bulwark):
+9
Willpower (master):
+6

Skills:

  • Trained: Survival +2, Diplomacy +4
  • Expert: Performance (orcish banjo) +6, Stealth +5
  • Master:
    Athletics +10, Intimidation +10*, Warfare Lore +7



    (* this includes the +2 from intimidating prowess)

Combat Actions:

  • 1 Action: Greatsword Attack:
    +12 to hit, 2d12+4 damage
  • 1 Action: Composite Longbow Attack: +9 to hit, 2d8+2 damage, Range
  • 1 Action: Instant Opening: Make 1 foe within 30 feet flat-footed to you for 1 round
  • 1 Action: Demoralize (+10): Make 1 foe within 30 feet frightened 1 or 2.


    • You don't need to speak their language, and if you strike them while they are demoralized the duration extends until the beginning of your next turn.
  • 1 Action: Glory and Valor: 1x a day, for 1 minute (or until you crit fail a strike) if you hit an equal foe you heal 5 hit points.
  • 2 Actions: Power Attack:
    +12 to hit, 4d12+4 damage
  • 2 Actions: Annihilating Swing: Once an hour, you can strike and destroy any nonmagical item, or up to 5 cubic feet of matter. If you strike a magical surface/item, you can attempt to counteract it.
  • Reaction: Stand Still: Attack a foe a who moves near you.
  • Reaction: Cleave: Attack a foe if you fell another foe
  • Reaction: Undying Ferocity: 1x a day, if you would be dropped to 0hp, drop to 1 instead and gain 10 temporary hit points.

Combat Features:

  • Iron Born: Reduce any speed penalties you take by 5 feet.
  • Knight Vigilant: You provide standard cover by being between ally and enemy

Non-Combat Features:

  • Lead Climber: When others Follow you as the Expert, if any of them critically fail their checks to Climb, you can attempt an Athletics check against the same DC. If you succeed, your ally fails instead of critically failing.
  • Underwater Marauder : You are not
    flat-footed
    while in water, and you don't take the usual penalties for using a bludgeoning or slashing melee weapon in water.
  • Forced Entry: You don't take a penalty to
    Force Open
    doors, windows, or containers without a crowbar. If you roll a success on a check to Force Open, you get a critical success instead.
  • Second Skin: You can rest armor without becoming fatigued
  • You can carry 7 Tokens (consumable magic items) without the danger of backlash.

Ancestral Complications:

  • Social Stigma: (Outcast) You belong to a group that most of the setting's society view as suspicious or lesser than. You get -2 to reaction rolls with most people.
  • Code of Honor: (Brigands) You have a very specific set of principles you follow all the time. These are the sort of principles you are willing to fight to the death over, not just pay lip service to.


    • Always avenge an insult, no matter the danger
    • Your friends' friends are your friends
    • Never attack a friend or member of your gang except in a fair, open duel

Background Complications:

  • Vulnerability: Due to a recessive lycanthropic gene from an ancestor, you take 5 points of extra damage from silver weapons.
  • Motion Sickness: When at sea or in another rocking, swaying or similarly moving vehicle, you are
    sickened
    1.
  • Bloodlust: You want to see your enemies die. You must make a
    self-control roll
    to use any non-lethal attacks against your enemies, whenever you need to accept a surrender, avoid a foe, take a prisoner or the like. If you fail that self control roll, you attempt to kill them instead, complications be damned.
