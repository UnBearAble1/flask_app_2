Here for the rules on Weapon Critical Specialization Effects

Weapon Traits


  • Agile: Reduced multiple attack penalty
  • Attached: Attached to another item, such as a shield
  • Backstabber: +1 precision damage to flat-footed targets
  • Backswing: +1 to hit after a miss
  • Brutal: Ranged weapon uses STR instead of DEX to hit
  • Climbing: You can climb with the same hand you hold this in
  • Concealable: +2 to checks to conceal
  • Deadly: +2 weapon dice of the listed size on critical
  • Disarm: Usable and useful when disarming
  • Fatal: On a crit increase damage to die size and add one dice
  • Finesse: Can use DEX instead of STR to attack (not damage)
  • Forceful: Get +2/+4 damage on subsequent attacks
  • Free-Hand: Can still use the hand this weapon is in
  • Grapple: Usable and useful to grapple with
  • Hampering: Can create difficult terrain for an action
  • Jousting: +2 damage, can use one handed when mounted
  • Modular: Can configure into different traits
  • Monk: These weapons are commonly used with ki-arts
  • Nonlethal: Deals nonlethal damage
  • Parry: Get +1 AC by spending an action
  • Propulsive: Add half your STR to damage rolls
  • Range: Can be used at the listed range
  • Ranged Trip: Can be used to trip at the listed range
  • Reach: Can be used up to 10 feet away
  • Reload: Takes the listed number of actions to reload
  • Repeating: Reload is for a set magazine of shots
  • Resonant: Can channel energy damage from spells
  • Shove: Usable and useful to shove with
  • Splash: Deals some damage adjacent targets
  • Sweep: +1 to het secondary targets in a turn
  • Tethered: Pull thrown weapon back with an interact action
  • Thrown: Can be thrown the listed feet
  • Trip: Usable and useful to trip with
  • Twin: +2 damage with attack from twin weapon
  • Two-Hand: Increased damage value when used with two hands
  • Unarmed: Uses your body instead of a weapon
  • Versatile: Can choose a different type of damage listed
  • Volley: -2 to hit targets within the range listed
