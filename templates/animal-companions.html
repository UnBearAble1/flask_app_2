Animal Companions


Some adventurers travel with loyal allies known as animal companions and familiars. The former begin as young animals but acquire impressive physical abilities as you level them up with feats, while the latter share a magical bond with you.

Your animal companion has the minion trait, and it gains 2 actions during your turn if you use the Command an Animal action to command it; this is in place of the usual effects of Command an Animal, and you don't need to attempt a Nature check.

If your companion dies, you can spend a week of downtime to replace it at no cost. You can have only one animal companion at a time.

You or an ally can ride your animal companion as long as it is at least one size larger than the rider. A companion can only use its land speed (and even then, that is halved) while carrying a rider. It also cannot use its Support Ability while carrying a rider. The Mount special feature reduces some of these restrictions.


Typically when you get the Animal Companion ability (usually through the Animal Companion feat) your companion begins as a Young Animal Companion. Additional feats may transform the companion into a Mature Animal Companion or further into an Incredible Animal Companion. These changes increase the stats and capabilities of the companion, as outlined in the appropriate feats.

When you first gain a Young Animal Companion, it's base abilities are determined by whether you choose a Brute Companion or a Skirmisher Companion (this choice cannot be changed later):

BRUTE COMPANION

Size: Medium or Large
Perception +7; low-light vision
Languages: -
Skills: Acrobatics +3 (trained), Athletics +6 (expert)
Saves: Fortitude +8, Reflex +6, Willpower +5
AC 16,
HP 65

Actions:

  • Melee Single Action, Primary Attack +7 [+2/-3] Damage 2d8+3

SKIRMISHER COMPANION

Size: Small or Medium
Perception +7; low-light vision
Languages: -
Skills: Acrobatics +6 (expert), Athletics +3 (trained)
Saves: Fortitude +6, Reflex +8, Willpower +5
AC 18, HP 50

Actions:

  • Melee Single Action, Primary Attack +7 [+2/-3] Damage 2d8+1
  • Melee Single Action, Secondary Attack (finesse, agile) +7 [+3/-1] Damage 2d6+1
Your companion has a lot of elements that require your customization, the first of which is it's appearance. Work with your GM for what is appropriate for the setting, but know that your companion's appearance does not impact its in-game statistics (unless the GM determines in some cases it should to preserve immersion, such as requiring a companion with a climb speed to have some explanation for how it can climb.) These selections, normally, cannot be changed later.

Step One: Size: The first element to choose is your companion's size (Medium or Large for Brute, and Small or Medium for Skirmisher.) Remember that size impacts your companion's lifting and encumbrance.



Step Two: Damage Type:
Determine the type of damage your companion's Primary (and, if Skirmisher, secondary) attack deals: Bludgeoning, Piercing, or Slashing. These should align with the type of attack you imagine for the creature: claws typically do slashing, bites piercing, slams bludgeoning - but more obscure natural attacks are possible, such a stabbing proboscis that deals piercing.



Step Three: Movement: Pick one of these sets of movement types for your companion:

  • Speed 35 feet
  • Speed 25 feet, Climb 25 feet
  • Swim 40 feet
  • Speed 30 feet, swim 15 feet
  • Speed 25 feet, swim 25 feet
  • Speed 25 feet, burrow 10 feet, climb 10 feet
  • Speed 20 feet, climb 20 feet, swim 20 feet


Step Four: Skills: Select an additional skill known by your companion:

  • Intimidation (expert)
  • Survival (trained)
  • Thievery (trained)


Step Five: Special Features: Select either 2 lesser special features or 1 greater special feature:

SPECIAL FEATURES

LESSER:

  • Mount: Your companion can move and support you in the same turn, and can move at its full land speed.
  • Scent (30 feet)
  • Aquatic Predator: Your companion has the aquatic trait (meaning it cannot breath on land, but only in water). Its blood scent enables it to smell blood in the water from up to 1 mile away.
  • Hardy: Your companion gains a +1 status bonus to all saves against disease, and if it rolls a success on a save against a disease, the result is a critical success instead.
  • Amphibious

GREATER:

  • Echolocation (20 feet) (can use hearing as a precise sense within this range)
  • Flyer: Replace your companion's movement types with: Speed 10 feet, fly 40 feet.

    • Limited to Small and Medium skirmishers only
    • Cannot fly if mounted or if lifting more than one bulk, excluding any barding designed for them.
  • Sneak Attack: 1d6 extra precision damage against flat-footed targets.
  • Darkvision
  • Venomous: The companion's primary strike deals an extra 1d4 poison damage


Step Six: Support Ability:
Select one Support Ability. In order to use the Support Ability, it must use the Support action:


  • Support (Single Action)

    • Requirements The creature is an animal companion.
    • Your animal companion supports you. You gain the benefits listed in the Support Benefit entry. If the animal uses the Support action, the only other actions it can use on this turn are basic move actions to get into position to take advantage of the Support benefits; if it has already used any other action this turn, it can't Support you.

SUPPORT ABILITY


  • Frightening: threatens your foes with menacing growls. Until the start of your next turn, if you hit and deal damage to a creature in your companion's reach, the creature becomes frightened 1.
  • Rutting: Your companion digs around your foe's position, interfering with its footing. Until the start of your next turn, if you hit and deal damage to a creature your companion threatens, the target can't use a Step action (unless it can Step through difficult terrain) until it moves from its current position.
  • Distracting: Your companion gets in the way of your enemies' attacks. Until the start of your next turn, creatures in your companion's reach that you damage with Strikes take a -1 circumstance penalty to their attack rolls.
  • Mauler: Your companion mauls your enemies when you create an opening. Until the start of your next turn, each time you hit a creature in the companion's reach with a Strike, the creature takes 1d8 slashing damage from the companion.
  • Destabilizing. Your companion constantly pushes your enemies. Until the start of your next turn, your Strikes that deal damage either make the target flat-footed until the end of your next turn or move the target 5 feet away from the companion (this is forced movement). The target chooses which effect occurs.
  • Gory: Your companion gores your foes. Until the start of your next turn, your Strikes that damage a creature in your companion's reach also deal 1d6 persistent bleed damage.
  • Eye-Poker: Your companion pecks at your foes' eyes when you create an opening. Until the start of your next turn, your Strikes that damage a creature that your companion threatens also deal 1d4 persistent bleed damage, and the target is dazzled until it removes the bleed damage.
  • Spitter: Your companion makes a ranged attack to spit at a foe within 10 feet. On a hit, the foe is dazzled for 1 round.
  • Teamwork: Your companion assists you in battle. You gain a +1 circumstance bonus on your next attack roll to Strike a foe within your companion's reach. The bonus lasts until the first time you use it or until the beginning of your next turn, whichever comes first.
  • Prop Up: Your companion helps you stay afoot, leaning into you and propping you up as you maneuver your opponent. Until the end of your next turn, as long as you are adjacent to your companion, if you attempt to Disarm, Shove, or Trip an opponent and roll a critical failure, you get a failure instead. You also gain a +2 to resist those same maneuvers.
  • Covering: Your companion makes distracting motions. Any creature that would gain lesser cover from the companion gains standard cover instead.
  • Off Balancing: Your companion throws your enemies off-balance when you create an opening. Until the start of your next turn, your Strikes that deal damage to a creature within your companion's reach make the target flat-footed until the end of your next turn.
  • Powerful Jaws: Your companion clamps its jaws on your foe, refusing to let go. Until the start of your next turn, if your Strike damages a creature in your companion's reach, your companion can latch onto the creature. While thus attached, the companion can move along with the target whenever the target moves until the end of your next turn. Your companion can latch onto only one creature in this way, and it must release the creature to make a Strike. If the target is smaller than the companion, it takes a -10-foot circumstance penalty to its Speeds and can't Fly while the companion is latched on.
  • Expert Flanker: Your companion constantly darts into flanking position. Until the start of your next turn, it counts as being in its space or an empty space of your choice within 10 feet when determining whether you and your companion are flanking; you can choose a different space for each of your attacks.
  • Riding Charge: (requires: Mount) Your companion adds momentum to your charge. Until the start of your next turn, if you moved at least 10 feet on the action before your attack, add a +2 circumstance bonus to damage. If your weapon already has the jousting weapon trait, increase the trait's damage bonus by 2 per die instead.
  • Blocker: Your companion moves its limbs in the way of enemy attacks. As long as you or your attacker is within 10 feet of your companion, you gain lesser cover from your companion against all attacks, not just ones where the companion would be in the way.
  • Presser: Your companion distracts an adjacent opponent with snapping jaws and rapid movements. Until the start of your next turn, if your Strike damages a foe, any time the foe attempts an action with the manipulate trait until the end of your next turn, it must attempt a DC 5 flat check. On a failure, the action is disrupted.
  • Release Particulates:
    Your companion releases a shower of dust or other particulates to reveal your foes. Until the start of your next turn, you ignore concealment on any enemy adjacent to your companion, and reduce the flat check to target hidden creatures to 7.
  • Fire Breather: Your companion snorts fire in your foes' faces. Until the start of your next turn, each time you Strike a creature in the companion's reach, the creature takes 1d6 fire damage from the companion.
  • Stinger: (requires venomous feature) Your companion drips poison from its stinger when you create an opening. Until the start of your next turn, your Strikes that damage a creature in your scorpion's reach also deal 1d6 persistent poison damage.
  • Constant Threat: Your companion is skilled at
    interfering with reactions. Until the start of your next turn, any creature your companion threatens can't use reactions triggered by your actions unless its has a threat rating of 8 or higher.
  • Sickening: Your companion deeply disturbs your enemies. Until the start of your next turn, if you hit and deal damage to the target, the target must succeed at a Fortitude save or become sickened 1 (sickened 2 on a critical failure). The DC is 18, or 20 if they are a specialized companion.
  • Maimer: Your companion tears tendons with each opening. Until the start of your next turn, your Strikes that damage creatures your companion threatens give the target a -10-foot status penalty to its Speeds for 1 minute (-15 on a critical success).

Additional Option: If you have a feat that allows you to select an Advanced Maneuver for your companion, select one from the following list:

ADVANCED MANEUVER


  • Frightening Display (Single Action)


    • Your companion makes a loud and frightening display, throwing a foe off balance. They attempt to Demoralize the target creature (with a +2 circumstance bonus); this display gains the visual trait and doesn't require language. While frightened by this ability, the target is flat-footed to your companion.

  • Rage (Single Action) - Concentrate, Emotion, Mental

    • Requirements: The companion
      isn't fatigued or raging.
    • The companion enters a state of pure rage that lasts for 1 minute, until there are no enemies it can perceive, or until it falls unconscious, whichever comes first. It can't voluntarily stop raging. While raging, the companion is affected in the following ways.

      • It deals 4 additional damage with its primary attacks and 2 additional damage with its secondary
        attacks.
      • It takes a -1 penalty to AC.
      • It can't use actions that have the concentrate trait unless they also have the rage trait. The animal companion can Seek even while raging.
      • After it has stopped raging, it can't use Rage again for 1 minute.

  • Thrash (Two Actions)


    • Your companion lashes out quickly at up to three adjacent foes. This attack deals 2d4+STR. Each attack counts toward the bat's multiple attack penalty, but the penalty only increases after all the attacks have been made.

  • Bear Hug (Single Action)


    • Requirements The companion's last action was a successful Strike.
    • The companion makes another Strike against the same target. If this Strike hits, the target is also grabbed, as if the companion had successfully Grappled the target.

  • Hustle (Two Actions)

    • The companion moves at incredible speed. It Strides three times.

  • Flyby Attack (Two Actions)


    • Requires Flyer feature
    • The companion Flies and makes a Strike at any point along the way.

  • Goring Charge (Two Actions)


    • The companion Strides twice in a straight line and then makes a Strike. As long as it moved at least 20 feet, it gains a +2 circumstance bonus to its attack roll.

  • Solid Footing (Two Actions)

    • Your companion strides
      twice with a +5-foot circumstance bonus to Speed, ignoring natural difficult terrain

  • Distracting Spray (Two Actions)


    • The companion sprays an adjacent target with its scent glands. The target must attempt a Fortitude save. Wiping off the musk takes an Interact action and ends the effect. The DC is 14+ the Companion's constitution modifier, or 16+ if they are a specialized companion.


      • Critical Success The creature is unaffected.
      • Success The creature is clumsy 1 for 1 round.
      • Failure The creature is clumsy 1 for 1 minute.
      • Critical Failure The creature is clumsy 2 for 1 minute.

  • Pounce (Single Action)


    • Your companion Strides and then Strikes. If it was undetected at the start of its Pounce, it remains undetected until after the attack.


  • Tongue Pull (Single Action)

    • Your companion
      snaps out its tongue, attempting to pull a foe within 15 feet toward it. The companion attempts an Athletics check against the foe's Fortitude DC. On a success, the companion pulls the foe next to it. This movement is forced movement. On a critical success, the foe is also grabbed until the beginning of your next turn.

  • Death Roll (Single Action)

    • Requirements The companion has a creature grabbed, or locked in its Powerful Jaws support feature
    • The companion tucks its legs and rolls rapidly, twisting its victim. It makes a Strike with a +2 circumstance bonus to the attack roll against the grabbed creature. If it hits, it also knocks the creature prone. If it misses, it releases the creature.

  • Darting Attack (Single Action) Flourish

    • The Companion Steps up to 10 feet and then Strikes, or Strikes and then Steps up to 10 feet.

  • Gallop (Two Actions)

    • The Companion Strides twice at a +10-foot circumstance bonus to Speed.


  • Gnaw (Single Action)

    • Requirements The animal companion's last action was a successful Strike.
    • The Strike's target takes 1d6 persistent bleed damage and takes a -10-foot status penalty to its Speed until it removes the bleed damage.


  • Careful Withdraw (Single Action)


    • Your companion Steps twice and then Strides.


  • Ultrasonic Scream (Two Actions)


    • Your companion emits ultrasonic squeaks that scramble the senses of nearby creatures. Choose one creature within 30 feet of the companion. The DC is 14+ the Companion's constitution modifier, or 16+ if they are a specialized companion.

      • Critical Success: The target resists entirely

      • Success The target treats all creatures as concealed on its next attack before the start of your next turn.
      • Failure The target treats all creatures as concealed until the start of your next turn.
      • Critical Failure As failure, and the target is also stunned 1.

  • Defensive Curl (Single Action)


    • The companion coils into a ball to defend itself. It gains a +2 circumstance bonus to AC. Any creature that hits the creature with a melee unarmed attack while it's in its Defensive Curl take 2d6 persistent bleed damage. The companion can't move, use its unarmed attacks, or take other actions that require it to use its limbs while in its Defensive Curl, but it can unfurl as a single action, ending the effects of Defensive Curl.

  • Breath Weapon (Two Actions)


    • Usable once per hour
    • The companion breathes a 30-foot cone of fire, dealing 5d6 fire damage to all creatures in the area (basic Reflex save). The DC is 14+ the Companion's constitution modifier, or 16+ if they are a specialized companion.


  • Grab and Sting (Two Actions)



    • Requires Stinger support feature and skirmisher type
    • Your companion makes a secondary attack against a creature.
      If the Strike hits, the target creature automatically becomes grabbed by the companion, which then makes a primary Strike against it (with normal multiple attack penalty). The grabbed condition lasts until the end of your next turn.

  • Shred (Single Action)

    • Requirements The Companion hit with a primary Strike on its most recent action this turn.
    • The Companion viciously rips into the wound, dealing additional damage. The target of the companion's Strike takes 2d6 slashing damage


  • Constrict (Single Action)

    • Requirements The companion has a smaller creature grabbed.
    • The companion deals 14 bludgeoning damage to the grabbed creature; the creature must attempt a basic Fortitude save. The DC is 18, or 20 if they are a specialized companion.


  • Tearing Strike (Two Actions)

    • Requires Skirmisher type
    • The Companion makes a secondary Strike; on a successful hit, the target takes 2d6 persistent bleed damage.


  • Feast on the Fallen (Reaction) Healing, Manipulate


    • Frequency once per hour
    • Trigger A creature adjacent to the companion is reduced to 0 Hit Points
    • The companion consumes a piece of the defeated foe, regaining 18 Hit Points. If the vulture is a specialized animal companion, increase the Hit Points regained to 30.

  • Knockdown (Single Action)

    • Requirements The animal companion's last action was a successful Primary Strike.
    • The companion automatically knocks the target of its Primary Strike prone.
