Exploration, Concentration, Secret
You take time surveying a small location (such as a single room) to get an impression of events that either occurred there within the last day, or surveying an object (such as a discarded cloak) to get an impression of what the item had been used to do, where it had been, or what clues can be uncovered from it, from within the last day. 

This involves moving about the area and studying footprints, the placement of objects, spilled drinks or blood, and so forth, or overturning and carefully examining the object thoroughly. 

  • Critical Success As success below, but you also are able to know which clue or piece of information is the most valuable to your investigation 
  • Success You get clues and details of the past, including the overall events and their time frame, but it's not a perfect record. This also isn't enough to identify who was involved in these events if you weren't already aware the person was there. As determined by the GM, you also pick out various seemingly small details that could serve as important clues, like a memorable weapon someone used for a murder or the type of cloak someone wore when passing through.
  • Failure You're unable to uncover any useful information 
  • Critical Failure As success, but you you get incorrect information
