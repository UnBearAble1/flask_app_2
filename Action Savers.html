These effects save you an action or actions. As the three action economy is a substantial part of how you will tactically behave in combat, reducing the number of actions an activity takes is a substantial boon.
Not all actions are equal. When calculating the action saver costs, unless otherwise specified, apply the following modifiers: 
  • Category A: Frequently Used Actions: Like Stride, Step, Strike, Reload, Take Cover or activating powers
  • Category B: Less Frequently Used Actions: Like Stand, Interact for a specific purpose like drawing a weapon or consuming a potion 
  • Category C: Rarely Used Actions: Like “Point Out” 
  • Skill Based Activities (Use the categories listed in Enhanced Activity Use)

Action Becomes Reaction

You turn a normal (non ability) activity into a reaction. Use the Situation Limit to set the trigger.   
  • 1 Action Converted: (D:  Mod: 2  | C: Base: 1  | B: Base: 4  | A:  Base: 8) 
    • To triggered free action: 20 points (D:  Mod: 4  | C: Base: 2  | B: Base: 6  | A:  Base: 10)
  • 2 Actions Converted: (D:  Mod: 4  | C: Base: 4  | B: Base: 8  | A:  Base: 12)
    • To triggered free action: (D:  Mod: 8  | C: Base: 8  | B: Base: 12  | A:  Base: 16)

Combined Activities


You combine two different, specific activities into a single action, saving you the cost of having to spend actions on one of them. You can only combine two activities this way, and they cannot be the same activity. 
  • 1 Action or reaction Saved: (D:  Mod: 2  | C: Base: 1  | B: Base: 4  | A:  Base: 8)
  • 2 Actions Saved: 80 Points (D:  Mod: 4  | C: Base: 4  | B: Base: 8  | A:  Base: 12)
  • Can use two exploration activities simultaneously (such as Avoid Notice and Cover Tracks): (D:  Mod: 1  | C: Base: 1  | B: Base: 3  | A:  Base: 6)
Note that this Combined Activities ability assumes that you are saving an action from a non-ability action. If you are trying to save actions by combining two abilities together, use the rules for Combined Abilities. 
  • Example: If you were adding a Stride to a custom defensive ability, allowing you to Stride and use the ability for a single action, you’d use this Action Saver feat to do so. If you were instead trying to combine a special movement feat with a special defense feat, you’d use the rules under Combined Abilities.

Extra Reaction

You have an extra reaction each round which can be used only for a specific activity, chosen at creation. 
  • Shield Block (Base: 2) 
  • Created Ability: (1/2 the final Base and Mod, round up) 

Quickened


Activation: Duration 

You gain the quickened condition, gaining an extra action. Reminder: Quickened applies when your actions refresh, so typically you gain no benefit the round you activate the quickened ability. 
  • Action can only be used to Stride, Step or Strike (Base: 16) 
  • Action can only be used for any other specific action: (D:  Mod: 8  | C: Base: 4  | B: Base: 8  | A:  Base: 12)
  • Action can be used for anything (Base: 20) 

Reduced Action Cost


You reduce the number of actions an activity takes. If this reduces an activity to 0 actions, it becomes a free action, though it can only be used once per round. Note that this is geared for non-ability activities. To reduce the activation speed of an ability, use the "Faster Activation" boost instead.
  • 1 Action Saved: (D:  Mod: 4  | C: Base: 4  | B: Base: 8  | A:  Base: 12)
  • 2 Actions Saved: (D:  Mod: 8 | C: Base: 8  | B: Base: 12  | A:  Base: 16)
