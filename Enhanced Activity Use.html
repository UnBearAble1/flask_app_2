Linked Skill: The most common skill used for the activity 

You have a particular advantage when performing an activity. By default, the advantage always applies when using the ability. The Base and Mod of this ability are based on how useful the activity is - with these as the defaults: 

Category D: Any specific mundane task that doesn’t normally require a check and isn’t tactically useful, such as painting fences, counting treasure, cooking food, etc.

Category C: (infrequent/low-impact): Balance, Command an Animal, Conceal an Object, Create Artwork, Create a Diversion, Decipher Writing, Grab an Edge, High Jump, Long Jump, Maneuver in Flight, Repair, Sense Direction, Squeeze, Swim, Stabilize Injuries,  Perform, Treat Disease, Treat Poison, Any specific mundane task that doesn’t normally require a check but could have tactical value, such as speaking, copying text from one source to another, reading a text, or donning armor.
 
Category B: (regular/useful): Aid, Climb, Cover Tracks, Discover Motivations, Earn Income, Escape, Force Open, Gather Information, Identify Magic / Alchemy, Impersonate, Lie, Palm an Object, Request, Steal, Subsist

Category A: (frequent/high-impact): Administer First Aid, Coerce, Craft, Disable Device, Hide,  Make an Impression, Notice, Persuade, Perform Surgery, Pick a Lock, Recall Knowledge (specific skill), Seek, Sense Motive, Sneak, Track

When you purchase this ability, you select one activity from the list above, and one benefit form the list below. 
Base Cost by Benefit: 

D:  Mod: 1  | C: Mod: 2  | B: Base: 1  | A:  Base: 2
  • Try Again: If you are using an activity that has a rule indicating you may not attempt the skill again upon a failure or critical failure (or if the DM adjudicates that you are unable to) you may attempt this action again once. If instead you'd get a penalty or difficulty increase for an additional attempt, you don't get that penalty on your second attempt. This ability does not lesson or undo any other results of your failure or critical failure. 
  • Beyond your Reach: You can attempt this activity even if you are untrained and training is a requirement.
 
D:  Mod: 2  | C: Mod: 3  | B: Base: 2  | A:  Base: 3
  • It'll buff right out: If you critically fail or fail a non-secret skill, you can spend the normal amount of time to perform the activity again (don’t reduce the time even if normally you would) to remove the consequence of that roll. Note, this doesn’t convert the roll to a success, just removes the specific consequence of failure to reset you back to before you even tried. Once done, you cannot attempt the skill roll again in the same exact situation until your next daily preparations. 
  • No Tool Requirement: You don't need a tool or kit to perform the action (assuming the action normally requires one), or aren't penalized for the absence of the tool or kit.
  • Balanced Skill: You are not flat-footed when performing the activity, assuming you normally would be
 
D:  Mod: 3  | C: Base: 1  | B: Base: 3  | A:  Base: 5
  • Assurance: You cannot fail Intermediate (DC 5 or lower) challenges, but can still critically fail on a natural 1, unless you also have the Cover Mistakes feature in this list. 
  • Cover Mistakes: You treat Critical Failures as Failures  
  • Unusual Skill, Narrow: You can use a non-standard skill to perform this activity (the non-standard skill becomes the linked skill). You can possibly substitute a skill for multiple variations of activities if they are similar enough (such as substituting one knowledge skill to identify magic from all other traditions.)
 
D:  Mod: 4 | C: Base: 2  | B: Base: 4  | A:  Base: 7
  • Shining Example: You treat successes as critical successes  
  • Second Chance: You can re-roll critical failures as a reaction  
  • No Hands: You can perform this ability even if your hands are full, assuming you normally couldn’t
  • Quick Skill: You can perform an activity that takes more than 1 hour in half the time. Additionally, even if it would normally take less than 1 hour, you can perform an exploration activity such as Cover Tracks or Avoid Notice while moving at full speed. To reduce the number of actions for encounter activities, see the “action saver” feat. 
 
D:  Mod: 5 | C: Base: 3  | B: Base: 6  | A:  Base: 9
  • Extra Effort: You can re-roll this activity using a reaction. You must keep the new result, even if it is worse.  
  • Swift Skill: You can perform an activity that takes 10 minutes or less with only 2 actions. 
  • Great Assurance: You cannot fail intermediate or moderate challenges (DC 8 or lower), but can still critically fail on a natural 1, unless you also have the Cover Mistakes feature in this list.
 
D:  Mod: 6 | C: Base: 4  | B: Base: 8  | A:  Base: 12
  • Skill Paragon: You roll this action with advantage 
  • Advanced Technique: Improve your results with this activity by one step 
  • Instant Skill: You can perform the activity instantly (typically exploration activities only, things that take 30 minutes or less, and not applicable to movement or contested abilities- to reduce number of actions for encounter activities, see the “action saver” feat)  
  • Know Your Weaknesses: This action isn’t a secret roll for you 
  • Unusual Skill, Broad: You can use a non-standard skill to perform this activity (the non-standard skill becomes the linked skill), and have a broad spectrum of applications (for example, you may be able to use Arcana to make any recall knowledge checks about magic at all). 
 
D:  Mod: 10 | C: Base: 8  | B: Base: 12  | A:  Base: 16
  • Perfect Technique: You cannot fail Intermediate, Moderate, or Difficult (DC 11 or lower), but can still critically fail on a natural 1, unless you also have the Cover Mistakes feature in this list. 

Editor's Note:
Use the “Situational” limit the application of your benefit, such as only gaining a bonus to Persuade only when convincing a target of the truth.
