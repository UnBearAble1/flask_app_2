These effects are all related to movement types, both in and out of combat. For adding extra movement in combat, including combining actions (such as allowing a Stride and Strike with a single move for a charging attack) consider the Action Saver effect. Additionally, if you are looking at a movement type that normally requires a check (Jumping, Swim, Squeeze, Tumble Through, Climb), consider the Enhanced Activity Use effect.

Burrow


[Base: 8]
You have a burrow speed of 15 feet. You can burrow through dirt, soil, sand, mud or the like, but not stone or harder materials. You leave no tunnels behind, but do leave signs that you burrowed.  
  • Your burrow speed is equal to your land speed (+60 points)
  • You can burrow through stone (+2 Base Tiers, +140 points)

Traversing Line


[Base: 8]
Activation, Duration 

You are able to launch a traversing line (enchanted rope arrows, webs flung from your wrists, vines that fly from your hands, etc), which affixes solidly to a surface, is attached to your body, and functions as a rope, meaning it can be climbed or swung from. The default range (and thus length of line) is 30 feet. The line can hold up your body weight plus 500 additional pounds. You can only have two lines in existence at one time, but you can release them from you at any time. You cannot end the existence of the line before the duration is up unless it also has the sustained or concentration limit. The line can be pulled free with an Interact action and a successful DC Athletics check.

Modification: 
  • You can retract the traversing line, pulling yourself toward its anchoring point. Each action you spend moving this way has the move trait, and moves you your land speed. [Base +4]

Climb Effects


Climb Faster 
Linked Skill: Athletics 
You can climb faster than normal. 
  • You climb 5 additional feet on a success, 10 on a critical, to a maximum of your speed (Mod: 2) 
  • You have a climb speed equal to half your land speed (Base: 4, mod +8) 
  • You have a climb speed equal to your land speed (Base: 8, Mod +8) 


Wall Stride 
[Base: 8, Mod +4] 
Linked Skill: Athletics
You can stride up vertical surfaces. You must start your movement on a horizontal surface. During this movement, you can run up vertical surfaces, like walls, at your full Speed. If you end the Stride off the ground, you fall after taking your next action or when your turn ends, whichever comes first (though you can Grab an Edge, if applicable). Note that non-smooth vertical surfaces may often be difficult terrain.

Linked Skill: Athletics

Incorporeal Effects


Become Incorporeal 
[Base: 16, Mod +24] 
Linked Skill: Thievery
Activation: Duration
You can pass through solid objects, including walls. When inside an object, an incorporeal creature can’t perceive, attack, or interact with anything outside the object, and if it starts its turn in an object, it is slowed 1. Corporeal creatures can pass through an incorporeal creature, but they can’t end their movement in its space. Being incorporeal this way by default does not protect you from physical attacks, so consider adding effects that do.


Meld Into Matter 
[Base: 8, Mod +16]
Linked Skill: Stealth
 Activation: Duration
You can only become incorporeal enough to meld into an adjacent piece of matter with a volume enough to hold your person (and gear). You can then unmeld from the matter, but you cannot use this version of incorporeal to travel further through the matter. While melded in this way, you can hear what is happening outside the matter, but can use no other senses, and you are not slowed 1. You can use any effects that do not require line of effect. If the matter you are hidden in takes significant enough damage, you are expelled and take 10d6 force damage.

[Mod: 2]
